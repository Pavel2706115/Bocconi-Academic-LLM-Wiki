## _IIIC-7. Computation of NC net income_ 

In the case of a non-wholly owned subsidiary: 

1. Assets are recognized at 100% in the consolidated financial statements. 

2. Liabilities are recognized at 100% in the consolidated financial statements. 

3. Equity includes a separate line for non-controlling interests (NCI).

## _IIIC-7. Computation of NC net income_ 

Recall that shareholders’ equity includes at least: 

1. Common stock 

2. Retained earnings (from prior periods) 

3. Profit/loss for the year 

Non-controlling interests are allocated items (i) and (ii) during the investment elimination. 

**The final consolidation adjustment consists of allocating to non-controlling interests their share of (iii) profit/loss for the year.**

## **WE USE A STANDARDIZED FORMULA APPROACH** 

Although no standardized formula is perfectly accurate, remember that “real” dividends for non-controlling interests are based on the subsidiary’s profit in its separate financial statements.Therefore, the allocation of profit to non-controlling interests in consolidated financial statements is only indicative and follows a simplified, standardized approach.

## _IIIC-7. Computation of NC net income_ 

To compute the NCI share of income, formula is as follows: 

# **NCI NI = (PrSub +- Adj) * %NCI** 

whereas 

1. NCI NI = Net Income attributable to Non Controlling Interest 

2. PrSub = Net profit realized by the subsidiary in its separate financial statements 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

3. Adj = adjustments to net profit due to consolidation entries, whereas the adjustments pertain the subsidiary ( _see next slide_ ) 

4. %NCI = percentage owned by the minorities in the common stock of the subsidiary 

_Example: minorities own 10%, profit of the subsidiary is 900 and adjustments are - 150_

## **Adjustments to take into consideration** 

The adjustments to consider are the following: 

1. Adjustments arising from depreciation/amortization of fair value surpluses, 

2. Adjustments from upstream transactions (i.e., transactions where the Subsidiary is the seller/provider and the Parent is the buyer/beneficiary). 

- Once the NCI share of net income is determined, the consolidation adjustment is: **1. Decrease “net profit” in the income statement** 

**2. Increase “NCI share of income” in the income statement**

**3. Decrease “net income” in equity** 

**4. Increase “NCI share of income” in equity**
