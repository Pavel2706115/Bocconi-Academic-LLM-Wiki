## _IIIC-6. Elimination of intercompany dividends_ 

When a subsidiary (Target) pays dividends to its parent (Acquirer) during an accounting period, the journal entries recorded by the two entities are as follows: 

SUBSIDIARY 

Retained earnings (- SE) 

Cash    (- A) 

PARENT 

Cash (+ A) 

Dividend revenue (+ Rev) 

The subsidiary’s journal entry has no impact on its income statement. The parent’s journal entry affects its income statement. 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

Dividends are taxed only on a small portion (e.g., 5%); therefore, for consolidation purposes, we assume that taxation on dividends is zero (this assumption is used both in this course and in practice). 

In light of the above, what consolidation adjustments are required? We distinguish between two cases: a wholly owned subsidiary (requiring only one adjustment) and a non-wholly owned subsidiary (requiring the same adjustment plus an additional one).

## **NON wholly owned** 

- In the case of a **NON wholly owned subsidiary** , 

- Retained earnings of the Target decrease for a certain amount (example: 200 €) 

- Revenues accounted by the Parent (so, INFRAGROUP REVENUES) are worth only the % owned in the subsidiary (example: 130 € if the parent owns 65% of the shares issued by the subsidiary). 

- Consequence: revenues decrease by % owned by the parent and retained earnings increase same amount. 

- NEVERTHELESS, THIS IS NOT ENOUGH.
