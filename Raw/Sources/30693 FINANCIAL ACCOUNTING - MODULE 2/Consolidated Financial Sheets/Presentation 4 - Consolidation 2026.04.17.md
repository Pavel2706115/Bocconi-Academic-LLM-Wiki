## _IIIC. Consolidation adjustments_ 

- ❑ The consolidated financial statement provides the representation of the economic and financial situation of the group as a single entity: a company with many divisions/internal functions (set up by different companies that are included in consolidation area). 

- ❑ Transactions that occur between group companies are equivalent, in view of the consolidated financial statement, to transactions between divisions/functions within a company. 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

- ❑ Such transactions, therefore, since are not transaction with “third parties”, should not even be recognized in the general accounting system. 

- ❑ The consolidated financial statement should represent only those operations that group companies have made with third parties outside the group. Those values, which arise from intra-group transactions, must be eliminated.

## _IIIC-3. Elimination of intercompany transactions_ 

- ❑ Trading and financing operations within a group of companies generate payables and receivables, as well as costs and revenues. 

- ❑ From the group’s perspective, treated as a single entity, these items are not relevant (the group has equal amounts of receivables and payables, and equal costs and revenues). They also artificially inflate the balances. 

- ❑ Therefore, these items must be eliminated in the consolidated financial statements. 

In particular, the following should be removed: 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

a. intra-group revenues and expenses recognized during the financial year; b. Intra-group receivables and payables outstanding at year-end. 

- ❑ Eliminations of intra-group receivables and payables, as well as costs and revenues, are performed at 100%, regardless of the presence of non-controlling interests (i.e., even when the parent does not fully own the subsidiary).

## _IIIC-3. Elimination of intercompany transactions_ 

- ❑ The procedure for eliminating intra-group transactions is based on the assumption that there is consistency between the accounts of each company. 

- ❑ This consistency exists if all group companies have correctly recorded such transactions. If, due to errors or incomplete information, this consistency is lacking, intra-group balances must be reconciled to ensure they are properly recognized by all companies. 

- ❑ After reconciliation, the elimination can be carried out. 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

- The logical steps to follow in adjusting and eliminating intercompany receivables and payables, as well as revenues and expenses, are as follows: 

- 1) identify the amounts of receivables and payables, and revenues and expenses arising from intragroup transactions, recorded in the financial statements of the companies included in the consolidated financial statements; 

- 2) ensure there is consistency between the accounts; if not, reconcile the intra-group balances; 3) eliminate the corresponding accounts (receivables and payables, revenues and expenses).

## _IIIC-4. Elimination of intercompany profits on sale of goods_ 

- ❑ In the consolidated financial statements, the group’s result should reflect only transactions with third parties, not those arising from transactions between group companies. 

- ❑ Therefore, any intra-group profits and losses related to assets still held within the group at year-end must be eliminated. 

- ❑ This consolidation adjustment aims to remove the effects of intra-group transactions, as if those transactions had never occurred. 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

- ❑ However, intercompany losses that represent a real decrease in the value of an asset should not be eliminated. 

- ❑ The elimination of intra-group transactions gives rise to temporary differences; therefore, deferred tax assets or liabilities arising from these differences must be recognized. 

- ❑ Adjustments to eliminate intercompany profits and losses are applied in full (100%).

The elimination of intercompany profits and losses is achieved by:

## _IIIC-4. Elimination of intercompany profits on sale of goods_ 

- ❑ In the case of profits (or losses) included in the value of inventories, intra-group profits (or losses) must be eliminated to the extent that they relate to goods still held in the acquirer’s inventory at yearend.

   1. calculate the **total intercompany profit (or loss) result** ;

3. reduce (or increase) the closing inventory balance by the amount of intercompany profit (or loss) not realized with third parties (2).

# ❑ The lower-of-cost-or-market rule requires an asset to be reported in the financial statements at the lower of its historical cost and its market value. 

- ❑ If the market value falls below its historical cost, the business unit writes down the value of the asset to its market value.

## _IIIC-4. Elimination of intercompany profits on sale of goods_ 

- ❑ It is possible that two companies within the same group enter into a transaction that gives rise to an intra-group loss. 

- ❑ In this case as well, the intra-group loss must be fully eliminated if it has not been realized with external parties. 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

- ❑ In any case, International Accounting Standards require that the underlying causes of the loss be examined. 

- ❑ If the loss is due to a long-term reduction in the asset’s value, the intra-group loss must not be eliminated, as it reflects a real decrease in the asset’s value.

## _IIIC-5. Elimination of intercompany profits on sale of longlived assets_ 

- ❑ In the case of profits (or losses) included in the value of long-lived assets, the following adjustments are required: 

   - ✓ report the transferred assets (still held by the acquiring company at year-end) at the carrying amount they would have had in the absence of the intercompany transaction; 

   - ✓ eliminate any gains or losses recognized as a result of the transfer; 

   - ✓ adjust depreciation so that it reflects the amounts that would have been recorded in the absence of the transfer. 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

- ❑ From a consolidated perspective, depreciation must be based on the cost of the asset to the group, i.e., the cost incurred by the company that originally purchased the asset from an external party. 

- ❑ Elimination entries are required in the consolidation workpapers to restate the asset, the related accumulated depreciation, and the depreciation expense to the amounts that would have been reported if the intercompany transfer had not occurred.

## _IIIC-5. Elimination of intercompany profits on sale of longlived assets_ 

- The logical process to follow for this adjustment is as follows: 

1. identify the accounting effects of the intercompany transaction, with particular reference to gains or losses, transferred assets, and their depreciation; these are reflected in the aggregated financial statements; 

2. determine the values that the affected items (gains or losses, transferred assets, and depreciation) would have had in the absence of the transaction; these are the values that should be reflected in the consolidated financial statements; 

**==> picture [50 x 144] intentionally omitted <==**

3. based on the differences between (1) and (2), identify the adjustments needed to convert the aggregated financial statements into consolidated financial statements. 

- With reference to losses on sales, the carrying amount of the asset must be compared with its value in use.
