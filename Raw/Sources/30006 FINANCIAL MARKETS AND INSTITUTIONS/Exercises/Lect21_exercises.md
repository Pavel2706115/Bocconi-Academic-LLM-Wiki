## Extra exercises of Lecture 21

## EX 1

Both bank X and Y have the same level of liabilities valued at $6,9 M, but bank X and Y total assets are valued at $7.5 M and at $7.7 M (respectively), and their capital is $600k and $800k (respectively). Which bank is riskier if risk-weighted assets are $2,5 M for both banks?

- a) It cannot be determined

- b) Bank X is as risky as bank Y

- c) Bank X is riskier than bank Y

- d) Bank Y is riskier than bank X

## EX 2

Bank A has the following assets: reserves of $100, treasury bonds of $200, residential mortgages of $400, and corporate loans of $50. Bank A's net worth is $100. Bank B has the following assets: reserves of $50, treasury bonds of $400, residential mortgages of $100, municipal bonds of $400 and corporate loans of $50. Bank B's net worth is $120. Assume the following risk weights apply: Reserves and High-quality government bonds 0%, High-quality government bonds and Residential mortgages 0% Residential mortgages and Municipal bonds 50% Corporate loans 100% Which bank will be considered riskier according to the Basel methodology?
*(See also: [[Lect20-21#^4k3ntt]])*

- a) both banks have the same level of risk b) the relative riskiness of the two banks cannot be determined from the information provided c) bank B is riskier

- d) bank A is riskier

## EX 3

A bank has deposits for which it pays an interest equal to id>0 and it has some positive capital (NW>0). The bank can invest in one of the two projects. A safe project A with a positive return RA> id and a project B with an expected positive return E(RB) such that with a given probability 0<pB<1 it yields RB, and 0 otherwise. Assume that deposits are fully insured by the government. If the return on project A increases of 20%, what happens to the capital requirement that makes the bank indifferent between investing in project A or B?

- a) it increases

- b) it decreases

- c) it does not change

- d) it is not predictable with the given data
