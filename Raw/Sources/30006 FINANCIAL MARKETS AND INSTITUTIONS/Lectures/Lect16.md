## Two main _**liabilities**_ 

## **1.** : **Currency in circulation** 

`o` banknotes and coins

## **2. Reserves** : 

- funds banks hold in a _deposit account_ at the Fed (Fed is like a bank for banks) + cash in bank vaults 

- Fed _guarantees_ that these deposits _can be redeemed_ for physical cash _at any point in time_ 

`o` Two types: 

▪ _reserves rrr_ = % _**Required**_ (RR): Fed sets the _required reserve ratio_ ( checkable deposits) banks must hold in cash at Fed: minimum 3-10% ▪ _**Excess** reserves_ (ER): any reserves beyond RRs

## **Normal times: open market operations** 

- ✓ Open market operations refer to either: 

   _1. purchase/sale of securities_ (T-bills) by the Fed from/to a set of banks (with **primary market dealers** ) 

   _2. discount window lending_ to financial institutions 

- ✓ The objective is to influence the amount of **money supply** (i.e. of **bank reserves** ) 

   - _purchase/sale_ of securities: reserves ↑/↓ 

   - reserves 

   - _borrowing/repaying from/to Fed:_ ↑/↓ 

   - so (mostly by trading _securities),_ Fed changes amount of reserves in the banking system 

- ✓ Bank reserves can be exchanged by banks in the **Federal Funds market** (a _secondary_ money market) 

- `o` which means _safe_ , short-term (overnight) instrument

`o` : (1) _open market operations_ **main refinancing operations** (like repos) via a bid system from its credit institutions (competitive bids before 2008, uncompetitive after) 

`o` (2) lending to banks: _marginal lending facility_ (ceiling of o/n in.t rate) `o` (3 _) deposit facility_ (floor of o/n int. rate) 

`o` (4) reserve requirements
