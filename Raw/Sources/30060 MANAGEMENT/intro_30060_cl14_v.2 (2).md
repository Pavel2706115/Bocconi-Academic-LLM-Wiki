LEARNING OBJECTIVES 

## Learning objectives 

- Understanding the basic concepts and theories of management 

- Make students aware of the complementary roles that different actors (individuals, organizations,..) play in modern economic systems 

- Develop a critical attitude regarding the objectives and the behavior of people engaged in business activities 

- Provide students with the fundamental concepts required for the economic analysis of firms' processes, as well as the ability to use them in basic situations 

- Provide basic tools to make decisions based on a scientific approach

TEACHING METHODS 

## Teaching methods 

- The course is designed in the purpose to combine concepts and theory with the richness of firms' real life. For this reason, students are strongly encouraged to take an active part in the learning process. 

- Case Study (i.e. Patagonia, Enron, Theranos, Apple,..) 

- Exercises (e.g. balance sheet, profit and loss, break-even point, ratios, economies of scale) 

- Polling, breakout-groups 

- Incidents, short stories, videos, business games …

GRADING SYSTEM 

## Grading system ATTENDING STUDENTS 

Attending students will be evaluated based on the following two parts: 

- A written exam (either one general exam or two mid-term exams): 80% of the final grade; 

- A field project: 20% of the final grade. 

## **WARNING** 

- The field project is an essential condition to be qualified as attending student, and hence to take part to mid-term exams. 

- However, students are free to not realize the field assignment, and give the general exam in December or later. 

- The field project grade has a validity of one academic year (up to the August 2025 exam).

GRADING SYSTEM 

## Grading system 

## ATTENDING STUDENTS 

## **WRITTEN EXAM** 

In order to take the written portion of the exam, students have two possibilities: 

- The first possibility is to take the mid-term exam at the end of October and the second part of the exam in December (for attending students only). If students pass the mid-term, they are admitted to the second part. Please note that the two mid-terms will be equally weighted (50% each) to compute the grade of the written part. 

- The second possibility is to take one single exam (general exam) either in December or in January/February (this option is compulsory for non-attending students); students that fail to pass the exam in the two regular sessions can give again the general exam in any other of the scheduled dates later in the academic year (March or August). 

- Students that have passed the mid-term exam in October may withdraw from the second part in December, by simply not registering for it – or not showing up on that day – and can directly take the general exam. Apart from this case, under no conditions a passed exam (or part of it) can be taken again in order to improve the grade.

GRADING SYSTEM 

## Grading system ATTENDING STUDENTS 

## **FIELD PROJECT** 

- Field projects represent a fundamental part of the learning process in a Management course. Hence, we strongly encourage to participate in a team project and qualify as attending student. 

## — Here are the basic rules: 

- Teams of six people will be formed by the instructors for the students who declare to be attending students (deadline to communicate your decision to be non attending students: September, Friday 27). 

- While attending the course is not compulsory, once a student gives his/her will to participate in a team, he/she should be able to complete the work to avoid creating problems to colleagues. For this reason, students that commit initially, but later defect from the group, will receive -1 on the overall final grade.
