FORMS OF ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE 

## Multidivisional Structure 

## **ADVANTAGES** 

- Delegation of decision making authority, allowing divisional and department managers to specialize 

- Better decisions are made faster, and tend to be more innovative 

- By focusing each division on a common region, product, or customer, each is more likely to provide products that meet the needs of its particular customers 

## **DISADVANTAGES** 

- It creates work duplication, which makes it more difficult to realize economies of scale that result from gruping functions together 

- Isolation among organizational areas

## Matrix Structure 

## **ADVANTAGES** 

- It provides flexibility, enhanced cooperation, creativity 

- Draws on very specific expertise 

- Develop general managers and reduces the burden of CEOs 

- It enables the company to react quicky to changes in the environment by giving special attention to specific projects/problems

## **DISADVANTAGES** 

- Matrix are generally expensive 

- Matrix are quite complex 

- Employees may be confused as to whose authority has priority: the project manager’s or the immediate supervisor’s? 

64 

## Team structure 

- Not hierarchical: it focuses on coordination rather than on control 

- Structure made of multiple work groups with weak or no authority lines between them 

- Little or absent work formalization; instead, freedom to design work as the teams like 

- Teams accountable for their performance 

- Good for employees engagement and motivation, flexibility and low barriers between knowledge areas 

- Team structures are often characterized by pressure to perform, have problems when scaling up, and may have unclear chain of command
