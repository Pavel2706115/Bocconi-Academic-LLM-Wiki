## The 6 Sources of Specialization Economies

- Activities take place with more speed and efficiently, with less effort, and with higher quality when they are specialized

- Learning economies through repetition

- Uneven distribution of skills (specialization according to skills)

## Line structure

- Simplest organizational structure

- Direct lines of authority extend from top management to employees at the lowest levels

Line structure Advantages: clear chain of command which enables managers to make decisions quickly

> Disadvantages: this structure requires that managers possess a wide range of knowledge and skills, as they are responsible for a variety of activities and must be knowledgable about them all

— A convenience store employee at 7 eleven report to an assistant manager, who reports to the store manager, who report to a regional manager (in case of an independent store directly to the owner)

- MOST COMMON in small businesses

## Line-and-Staff structure

- Traditional line relationship between superiors and subordinates

- Specialized managers – also called staff managers – assist line managers

- Staff managers do not have authority over line managers but over subordinates in their own departments

## Line-and-Staff structure

## ADVANTAGES

## DISADVANTAGES

Line managers can focus on the area of expertise in the operation of the business Staff managers provide advice and support on specialized matters such as finance, HR, engineering ^cvx5k6

## Multidivisional Structure

- Organizes departments into larger groups called divisions

- Corporate headquarter set the vision for the whole group

- Permits delegation of decision-making authority

- Inevitably creates work duplication

## Multidivisional Structure

## ADVANTAGES

- Delegation of decision making authority, allowing divisional and department managers to specialize

- Better decisions are made faster, and tend to be more innovative

- By focusing each division on a common region, product, or customer, each is more likely to provide products that meet the needs of its particular customers ^ud4rln

## DISADVANTAGES

- It creates work duplication, which makes it more difficult to realize economies of scale that result from gruping functions together

- Isolation among organizational areas

## Matrix Structure

- Sets up teams from different departments; creates two or more intersecting lines of authority

- The matrix superimposes project-based departments on the more traditional function-based departments

- — A matrix sets up teams from different departments, creating two or more intersecting lines of authority

- — NASA was one of the first to implement it because it needed to coordinate different projects at the same time.

## Matrix Structure

## ADVANTAGES

- It provides flexibility, enhanced cooperation, creativity

- Draws on very specific expertise

- Develop general managers and reduces the burden of CEOs

- It enables the company to react quicky to changes in the environment by giving special attention to specific projects/problems

## DISADVANTAGES

- Matrix are generally expensive

- Matrix are quite complex

- Employees may be confused as to whose authority has priority: the project manager’s or the immediate supervisor’s?

## Team structure

- Not hierarchical: it focuses on coordination rather than on control

- Structure made of multiple work groups with weak or no authority lines between them

- Little or absent work formalization; instead, freedom to design work as the teams like

- Teams accountable for their performance

- Good for employees engagement and motivation, flexibility and low barriers between knowledge areas

- Team structures are often characterized by pressure to perform, have problems when scaling up, and may have unclear chain of command
