## Liquidity 

- Short/Long-term solvency, which is the capacity for the company to pay all current liabilities 

- Short-term liabilities are an important way of funding firm’s operations (e.g. payables) 

- Profitability and liquidity are not the same concepts (It is possible to be profitable, having liquidity problems): 

   - Not all the payments are made at the same time (e.g. Delayed Payments) 

   - Not all inflows and outflows of cash are costs and revenues (e.g. Debt Reimbursement)

## Current ratio 

Current assets Current liabilities 

- A ratio greater than 1 tells us that the firm has enough near-cash assets to cover payments due in the immediate future 

   - However, as all indexes, the threshold value differs drastically depending on industry etc. 

   - https://www.readyratios.com/sec/ratio/current-ratio/ 

- It doesn’t distinguish between different types of current assets (some are more liquid than others)

LIQUIDITY RATIOS 

## Quick ratio 

Current assets – Inventories Current liabilities 

- We exclude inventories because its may be difficult sometimes to turn them quickly into cash 

   - The numerator includes only current assets that can become cash soon (e.g. cash holdings, marketable securities) 

- A value of 1 is very high, Around 0.8 is a good figure 

- Widely used by lending institutions to assess companies’ liquidity levels

LIQUIDITY RATIOS 

## Working capital to sales ratio 

Current assets – Current liabilities X 100 Sales 

- Includes items from both B/S and income statement 

- It measures the relationship between the funds used to finance a company's operations and the revenues a company generates → It says how efficiently a company is using its working capital to support sales and growth 

- Values between 10% and 20% are considered solid 

- Not necessarily co-move with current and quick ratio 

- Remember that the other formula to compute working capital is OF+LTD-FA

FINANCIAL LEVERAGE 

## Takeaway 

- Use financial leverage with caution 

   - A high use of financial leverage to deliver high ROE may also entail greater corporate risk 

   - Particularly risky in times of high uncertainty 

- Combine information from ROE with other indexes 

   - In particular, ROTA, which assesses the ability to generate profits from the entire asset base – not just equity) 

   - Also, use solvency indicators to better understand the capital structure of the company (too much debt?)
