## **Advertising** 

- A form of persuasive and often costly mass communication, originating from an identifiable source and aimed at achieving commercial objectives. 

Key characteristics: 

- _Means used_ : generally mass media; 

- _Persuasive component_ : influences motivations, attitudes, and behaviors; 

- _Cost: typically high_ ; 

- _Message source:_ clearly identifiable as the company; 

- _Purpose_ : commercial (i.e., not purely editorial*).

## **The [[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Communication-and-Advertising|advertising brief]]** 

A document that outlines the company's advertising strategy and objectives. It defines the target audience, the core message to be communicated, the tone of voice, and the channels through which the message will be delivered (intended recipient: the communications agency). 

The brief answers the following key questions: 

- **Who** is the target audience? (Who are we communicating to?); 

- **What** is the main message? (What are we communicating?) 

- **How** should the message be conveyed? (What tone, style, and approach will be used?) 

- **Which** communication channels are most appropriate for delivering the message?
