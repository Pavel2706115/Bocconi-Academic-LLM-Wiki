- **Text:**
    - _Institutional_ : communicates the company’s identity and overall image;

    - The [[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Communication-and-Advertising|advertising message]] can be broken down into four key components:
        - **1. Basic promise** (or main benefit): the core idea or value that the company aims to communicate to its target audience through advertising;
        - **2. Reason why**: the explanation of how the brand can deliver credibly on its promise – demonstrating reliability and relevance;

An advertisement is not just a random collection of words and images; it has four core components. First, the basic promise, which is the main consumer benefit. Second, the "reason why," explaining the mechanism of how the product works to deliver this benefit. Third, supporting evidence, providing external validation (like scientific data or expert quotes). Fourth, the tone of voice, which sets the emotional and cultural personality of the message.
