## **Distribution formats: the supermarket** 

_Retail_ outlet operating in the _food_ sector, organized as _a self-service store_ with payment at the exit, with a sales area _of between 400 and 2,499_ . _square meters_ 

## **Features** : 

- Wide range of fast-moving consumer goods (pre-packaged) and nonfood items for everyday household use; 

- Located in urban areas or smaller conurbations.

## **Distribution formats: hypermarket** 

_Retail_ outlet operating in the _food_ sector, organized as _a self-service_ store with payment at the exit, with a sales area _greater than or equal to 2,500_ . _square meters_ 

## **EVERYTHING UNDER ONE ROOF** 

## **Characteristics** : 

- wide range of non-food goods; 

- large car park; 

- large number of checkouts; 

- extended opening hours; 

- counters for fresh products with assisted sales (butcher, fishmonger, bakery, pastry shop); 

- large quantity and variety of branded products; 

- competitive prices and extensive use of price promotions.

## **Distribution formats: superstore** 

_Self-service retail_ outlets selling _food and non-food_ items; _floor space exceeds 2,000 square meters_ and generally _does not exceed 3,000 square meters_ . 

## **Features** : 

- Located in suburban areas easily accessible by public transport; 

- Independent points of sale with their own car parks or located in medium-sized shopping centers; 

- • Opening hours similar to supermarkets, with extended hours on public holidays; 

- • Expanded range in certain product categories, especially non-food (general goods and clothing).

## **Distribution formats: convenience stores** 

_Small_ retail outlets (a few dozen square meters) offering a range of _food and non-food_ products to meet everyday needs. 

## **Features** : 

- Extended opening hours: 24 hours a day, seven days a week; 

- Customers are mostly passing through and purchases are impulse or "emergency" purchases. 

- Some stores are also equipped to offer breakfast and readymade meals that can be eaten on the premises or at home.

## **Distribution formats: cash and carry** 

This represents the application of self-service to a wholesale warehouse → shops that are not accessible to private customers, but are intended for professional users such as shopkeepers and retailers. 

**Characteristics:** 

- Usually large in size; 

- Developed as stand-alone structures with ample parking; 

- Payment is generally in cash against immediate issuance of an invoice; 

- Customers are responsible for transporting the goods themselves.

## **Distribution formats: department stores** 

**==> picture [255 x 192] intentionally omitted <==**

_Retail_ business operating in the _non-food_ sector, with a sales area of more than 400 square meters and at least five _separate departments_ (in addition to any food department), each of which is dedicated to the sale of items belonging to different product sectors, most of which are fast-moving consumer goods. 

## **Characteristics** : 

- sales are usually assisted; 

- offers medium to high-quality products; 

- clothing is the main department; 

- the range is completed by semi-durable consumer goods for the home and leisure; 

- located in prestigious areas within important buildings; 

- no parking available; 

- includes dedicated catering spaces.

## **Distribution formats: sho in center pp g** 

- A complex of at least ten retail outlets with infrastructure, shared services, and parking. 

- Designed, promoted, built, and managed according to uniform criteria by specialised companies. 

- Forty percent of the total sales area is allocated to traditional and specialized shops. 

- The center must also be integrated with commercial activities (e.g. bars, restaurants, banks, post offices, business agencies) and possibly non-commercial activities (e.g. theaters, cinemas, conference rooms) in order to increase its appeal.

## **Distribution formats: Factory Outlet Centers (FOC)** 

• Facilities similar to shopping centers, containing retail outlets ( _company stores_ ) belonging to manufacturers operating mainly in the clothing and accessories sector. 

- They are traditionally established by large real estate companies and are located in strategic suburban areas due to their ability to attract a large number of customers. 

- They cover a very large area, generally exceeding 10,000 square meters, and their architecture is reminiscent of small villages with streets, squares, fountains, etc.

## **The structural evolution of the commercial sector** 

- _Modernization_ : the emergence of modern formats: 

   - USA, first half of the 20th century: supermarkets and department stores; 

   - UK, first half of the 20th century: superstores; 

   - Germany, 1960s: discount stores; 

   - France, 1970s-1980s: hypermarkets and large-scale retail trade;

   - Italy: modern formats spreading into the country in the 1990s. 

- _Concentration_ : increase in the size of commercial enterprises → increase in their power (super-central purchasing organizations) 

- _Internationalization_

## **The extension of core services** 

- Logistics services have traditionally been the raison d'être of commercial intermediares 

- Other types of services have become increasingly important over time 

   - → **EXTENSION OF THE CORE SERVICE** 

- which has led to ever-increasing investment in activities not traditionally carried out by retailers (e.g. catering, credit, leisure, fuel distribution, etc.)
