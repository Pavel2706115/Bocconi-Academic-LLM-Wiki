### Slide 11: B2C Segmentation Methods

**Slide elements (verbatim/structured):**

- **Title:** B2C segmentation
- **Text:**
    - **2 methods:**
        - **1. _ex ante_ or _a priori_**, in which the segmentation criteria refer to specific characteristics of the subject (which influence their preferences), attributable to different elements:
            - _socio-demographic_: age, gender, place of residence, income, level of education, etc.;
            - _behavioral*_: level of product purchases, level of brand repurchase, shopping habits, etc.;
            - _psychographic_: summarized according to so-called lifestyles.
        - **2. _ex post_ or _a posteriori_**, where the objective is to create homogeneous segments in terms of the benefits sought by the market, creating clusters of consumers who are sensitive to the same elements of the offer = benefit segmentation
    - _*e.g. "heavy" customers – heavy users – and "light" customers – light users_
- **Visuals (charts/tables/images):**
    - Picture of size 63 x 19 intentionally omitted.
- **Labels/footnotes:** "Slide number 11"

**Explanation of content:**

- **Text explanation:** This slide introduces the two main approaches to B2C (Business-to-Consumer) segmentation. Under the *a priori* (ex ante) method, segments are defined beforehand based on external consumer characteristics such as demographics, behaviors, or psychographics (lifestyles), assuming these characteristics drive purchasing preferences. In contrast, the *ex post* (a posteriori) method groups consumers based on their actual preferences and the specific benefits they seek from the product (e.g., cost savings, quality, convenience). Statistical techniques like cluster analysis are used on survey responses to uncover these segments after the research is done.
- **Graph/table explanation:** Not applicable.
- **Key takeaways from visuals:** Not applicable.

**What this slide is really saying (1–3 sentences):**

B2C segmentation can either be structured *a priori* based on pre-defined traits (like demographics or lifestyles) or discovered *ex post* based on the specific benefits consumers seek.

- **Text:**
    - # **advantages**
        - easy availability of secondary data
