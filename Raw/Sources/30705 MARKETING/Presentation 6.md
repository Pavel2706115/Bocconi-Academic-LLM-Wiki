## **B2C segmentation** 

2 methods: 

_1. ex ante_ or _a priori_ , in which the segmentation criteria refer to specific characteristics of the subject (which influence their preferences), attributable to different elements: 

      - _socio-demographic_ : age, gender, place of residence, income, level of education, etc.; 

      - _behavioral*_ :  level of product purchases, level of brand repurchase, shopping habits, etc.; 

      - _psychographic_ : summarized according to so-called lifestyles. 

_2. ex post_ or _a posteriori_ , where the objective is to create homogeneous segments in terms of the benefits sought by the market, creating clusters of consumers who are sensitive to the same elements of the offer = benefit segmentation 

   - *e.g. "heavy" customers – heavy users – and "light" customers – light users

## **Good segmentation** 

## meets **five** key requirements: 

_**1. measurability**_ : it leads to the identification of customer groups that can be precisely defined and quantified in terms of size, purchasing power, characteristics, etc.; 

_**2. importance**_ : it allows the importance of the different segments for the company to be established; 

_**3. differentiability**_ : it leads to the possibility of identifying a system of different preferences for the various clusters identified, rather than common or homogeneous ones; 

_**4. stability**_ : it leads to the definition of segments with lasting preferences for a period sufficient to guarantee a return on investment for the [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] actions that will subsequently be carried out; 

_**5. accessibility**_ : enables the company to reach and serve the segments under cost-effective conditions (where and how to talk to them) 

6.

## **[[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Segmentation-and-Positioning|Positioning]]** 

Referes to how a product is perceived by customers in relation to competitors and their ideal expectations. 

- →It plays a fundamental role in the evaluation phase of the [[Wiki/Concepts/Consumer-Behavior-Models|purchasing process]]. 

[[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Segmentation-and-Positioning|Positioning]] is expressed in terms relative to two benchmarks: 

- the _ideal product_ expressed by the target consumer segment; 

- the _competition_ , as perceived by the same demand segment. 

_A metaphor for positioning_ : this ‘placement’ is achieved through the implementation of the company's market policies which, as a result of appropriate commercial actions taken to differentiate its offer, succeed in [[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Segmentation-and-Positioning|positioning]] the company in a welldefined way in the perceptual demand space.

## **Improving current [[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Segmentation-and-Positioning|positioning]]** 

a) modify the offer; b) adjust the relative weights of the attributes–benefits (altering consumer priorities); c) change consumer perceptions of the company → psychological repositioning; d) change consumer opinions about competitors; e) draw consumers' attention to attributes that are not currently part of the process of evaluating alternative offers; 

- f) modify the perceived position of the ideal product to bring it closer to your own product; g) introduce a new product or brand aimed at a consumer segment that appreciates the ideal product not covered by any competitor → flanking strategy; 

h) introduce a highly innovative offering capable of completely changing the dimensions, perceptions, and ultimately the very structure of the market.
