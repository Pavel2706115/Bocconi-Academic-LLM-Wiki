## Meaning of range 

- _Commercial range_ : by including products from various industrial suppliers, a commercial business seeks to meet a broad or narrow spectrum of consumer needs as shaped by market demand 

- _Industrial range_ : the industrial offering is typically designed to address the specific needs of clearly defined customer segments.

## **2. The industrial company’s [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product range]] (4/5)** 

When defining the [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product range]], it is crucial to select the most appropriate **[[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|branding strategy]]** for [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] the product portfolio. The most common alternatives include: 

**1. Branded house** _(brand family)_ : all product lines are offered under a single brand. This approach benefits form brand consistency and recognition across multiple product categories; 

**2. House of brands** _(separate brands)_ : each product or [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product line]] is associated with a distinct brand, reducing the the risk that failure in one area will impact the others (e.g. Henkel owns _Percil_ for laundry detergents and _Shout_ for colour protection, but consumers may not perceive them as part of the same company); 

**3. Umbrella brand** ( _endorsed brand_ ): a hybrid approach where each [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product line]] has its own brand identity, but all are endorsed by a parent brand.

## **2. The industrial company’s [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product range]] (5/5)** 

_A product portfolio analysis_ can be conducted to evaluate the performance of the products within the company’s range. This makes it possible to monitor the _competitive position_ and _results_ of each product in terms of turnover, profitability, [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market share]], and other key metrics. 

_Product portfolio matrices_ classify products into different categories based on their performance and [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market potential]] This helps assess the overall _balance_ of the portfolio and identify opportunities to redefine or optimise the [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product range]].

## **3. The commercial enterprise's [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product range]] (1/4)** 

The [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product range]] is a core component of a commercial . enterprises’s _retailing mix_ 

It has the greatest impact on the company’s _image_ and _positioning_ within the distribution landscape. 

By increasing the _breadth_ of its [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|product range]], the enterprise aims to meet a broader set of general consumer needs within a single point of sale. 

Conversely, an increase in depth of the range serves to satisfy more specific and differentiated demand needs.

## **4. Product innovation (1/3)** 

Companies are increasingly driven to innovate due to rising competitive pressure, prompting them to explore new sources of value creation: 

_1. Internally_ , by improving management efficiency (e.g. through production optimization); 

_2. Externally_ , by enhancing customer orientation and continuously updating product differentiation.

Innovation is the foundation of competitive advantage. It enables companies to achieve "competitive isolation», which is essential for long-term survival.

## **4. Product innovation (2/3)** 

**The technology-driven perspective** Innovation occurs when a company successfully transforms technological and scientific opportunities into new products that better meet market demand. **The market-driven perspective** A product is considered new if it satisfies existing needs in a new way, or it addresses previously unmet needs.

A new product can be seen as **a connecting medium** between the realm of technological possibilities and the sphere of consumer needs.

## **4. Product innovation (3/3)** 

In the **early stages** of the new product development process, R&D managers are primarily involved, alongside [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] professionals. In the **middle stages** (prototype definition and industrialization),the focus shifts to production and technical managers. 

In the _,_ **final stages** , including market testing and product launch [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] professionals take the lead.
