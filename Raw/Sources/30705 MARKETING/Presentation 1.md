## **1. Trust as a key resource** 

Many changes often manifest themselves in the form of shocks, as in the case of the recent pandemic crisis, which has had such a profound effect on the behavior of consumers, businesses, and institutions. 

In such situations, people feel a strong need for certainty, and companies, in order to emerge stronger from such a turbulent environment, can no longer limit themselves to satisfying customer demands but must necessarily gain their trust over time. 

This gives rise to the need for ‘[[Wiki/Concepts/Sustainable-Marketing-and-Trust|trust-oriented marketing]],’ that is, [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] aimed at gaining maximum customer trust in the long term, binding them to the company in a stable and lasting relationship that brings tangible benefits to both companies and customers.

## **2. Different business orientations** 

To define what [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] is, reference is usually made to different business orientations: 

**1. product orientation** and **production** orientation, which focuses on the design and technical development of the product and its industrialization; 

**2. sales orientation,** which looks in particular at the needs of the seller and places the sales target or budget at the center of its activities; 

**3. [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] and trust orientation,** which places the customer and their trust in the company at the center of the company's activities. 

The latter orientation represents the final frontier of [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] management, which all excellent companies should strive for.

## **3. [[Wiki/Concepts/Sustainable-Marketing-and-Trust|Trust-oriented marketing]]** 

- A **[[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]]- and trust-oriented** company pays particular attention to two aspects of the environment in which it operates: 

- **individual customer:** understanding their needs and, secondly, aiming to **satisfy them** as fully as possible in order to win their long-term **trust** ; 

- **the competition:** in order to gain the lasting trust of customers, it is essential to act in such a way as **to be perceived as the best** in the market and to **distance** oneself decisively from competitors in perceptions of demand.

## **3. [[Wiki/Concepts/Sustainable-Marketing-and-Trust|Trust-oriented marketing]]** 

**Trust** has a number of significant consequences for the company: 

1. it increases the _**frequency of**_ customer _**purchases**_ ; 

2. it generates _**word-of-mouth recommendations**_ ; 

3. it increases purchases of higher quality goods ( _**trading up**_ ); 

4. it encourages purchases of other goods offered by the company ( _**crossbuying**_ ); 

5. it reduces _**price sensitivity**_ , making demand less rigid; 

6. it encourages _**greater consumption**_ by customers; 

7. it generates a _**reduction in costs**_ associated with sales and customer service activities; 

8. it promotes greater willingness on the part of customers to share information and knowledge with the company ( _**knowledge sharing**_ ).

## What does trust depend on, and what can we do to build it? 

Trust is determined by a series of factors that can essentially be traced back to (Figure 1.1): 

- _past experiences_ with the other party and the level of satisfaction achieved on those occasions; 

- _perceived skills and competences_ , which enable the company to act in line with demand expectations. In the case of a doctor, for example, we rely primarily on their scientific skills and experience; in the case of a car repair shop, we look first and foremost for the mechanical and technical skills of the employees; 

- the _motivations_ to pursue the other party's objectives without resorting to opportunistic behavior, an aspect often referred to as ‘benevolence.’ If we perceive an opportunistic tendency in a doctor to prescribe drugs or tests, our trust declines; similarly, we trust the repair shop we usually go to because, in addition to their technical skills, we recognize that they have an adequate level of integrity, which means that the final bill includes only the services actually performed; 

- the _frequency of relations_ between the parties and, above all, the frequency of communication between customer and supplier, making the relationship fluid and transparent (we tend to trust those who communicate often and openly much more than those who are less present and more cryptic in their exchanges); 

- the _integrity of the company_ and the values associated with its brands.

- not only the _economic interests_ and _expectations_ of its **shareholders** ; 

- but also the satisfaction of the interests of a wider range of 

   - **stakeholders** , such as _employees, suppliers, customers, financiers,_ . 

   - _the community, local communities, and so on_ 

- The goal is to achieve good results in terms of **the ‘triple bottom line** _**economic social** and_ ,’ that is, satisfactory performance in _,_ 

- _**environmental**_ terms. These results are reported in the _**sustainability report**_ or impact report.

## **7. The [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] plan** 

The **[[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] plan** is a _concise management tool_ that contains: 

- the salient elements of [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] processes, 

- the important aspects of the analyses and 

- the criteria underlying strategic or tactical decisions aimed at 

   - generating trust and loyalty among customers.

## **7. The [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] plan** 

- _**First section:**_ this includes a presentation of the company and its macro-objectives. 

- _**Second section:**_ this illustrates the analysis of the [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] situation (analytical [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]]), which is summarized in the SWOT analysis. 

- _**Third section:**_ this explains the [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] activities that the company intends to carry out in order to achieve its objectives. 

- All of this is accompanied by **an action plan** ( _scheduling_ ) and a _marketing cost_ **budget** , which are essential for carrying out _control activities_ .

## Business and [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] plan differences 

||**Business plan**|**[[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|Marketing]] plan**|
|---|---|---|
|When|Start-up|Every year|
|To Whom|External stakeholders (funders, public<br>institutions, etc.)|Internal stakeholders (general<br>management, other functions,<br>collaborators, etc.)|
|What|All activities related to the new<br>business (organization, production,<br>fixed investments*, finance, etc.)|[[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|Marketing]] strategy related to<br>one or all brand(s)/product(s)<br>and [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]] activities|



Strategic Plan - covers a period beyond the next fiscal year. Usually between 3 and 5 years 

Tactical Plan - covers the actions to be taken, by whom, during a short-term planning period (1 year) 

* i.e. capital goods
