**What this slide is really saying (1–3 sentences):**

It takes the total revenues (purchases frequency $Q_t$ multiplied by transaction margin/price $M_t$) and subtracts the initial acquisition cost $A_0$, as well as ongoing relationship development ($S_t$) and maintenance costs ($F_t$) over time.

### Slide 43: How to Calculate Net Promoter Score (NPS)

**Slide elements (verbatim/structured):**

- **Title:** How to calculate [[Wiki/Concepts/Sustainable-Marketing-and-Trust|Net Promoter Score]]
- **Text:**
    - A simple, immediate indicator for measuring the health of a brand and the propensity of consumers to spread the word.
    - Just one question: ‘Would you recommend brand XY to your friends, family, or colleagues on a scale of 0 to 10 (where 0 = absolutely not; 10 = definitely)?’
    - _**Detractors**_ (0 - 6 score)
    - _**Passives**_ (7 - 8 score)
    - _**Promoters**_ (9 - 10 score)
    - **NPS Formula:**
        $$\text{NPS} = \% \text{ Promoters} - \% \text{ Detractors}$$

- **Text explanation:** This slide introduces Net Promoter Score (NPS), the standard industry metric for measuring brand loyalty and customer advocacy. Based on a single question ("Would you recommend this brand on a scale of 0 to 10?"), it divides respondents into Detractors (0-6), Passives (7-8), and Promoters (9-10). NPS is calculated by subtracting the percentage of Detractors from the percentage of Promoters.
- **Graph/table explanation:** The omitted graphic shows the standard visual scale of NPS, highlighting the gap between Promoters and Detractors.

Net Promoter Score (NPS) is the ultimate metric for customer loyalty. It calculates the net percentage of passionate brand promoters by subtracting dissatisfied detractors.
