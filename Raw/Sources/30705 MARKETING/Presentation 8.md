- **Title:** INCIDENT 1 – Contribution margin
- **Text:**

    - The payment duration is 6 years.
    - The total variable cost for the company is €4,440 and the target contribution margin is 60%.
    - **What is the annual premium, assuming it remains constant?**
    - **Input:**
        - Unit contribution margin = $p - \text{Unit direct variable costs}$
        - Contribution margin % = $\frac{p - \text{vc}}{p}$
        - $\text{VC} = p \times (1 - \text{contribution margin \%})$

The contribution margin is the difference between price and unit variable cost, representing the percentage of each euro of sales that is available to cover fixed costs. Using the provided algebraic relationships, the lifetime premium is determined by dividing the total variable cost of €4,440 by the inverse of the contribution margin percentage $(1 - 0.6 = 0.4)$. To operationalize this, firms use multi-attribute evaluation models like the Fishbein model, which weights product attributes by importance and scores them. Comparing scores to a competitor yields a competitive index that determines how much of a premium or discount the firm can charge.
