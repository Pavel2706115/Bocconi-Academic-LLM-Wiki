## **The fundamentals of quantitative demand analysis (2/2)** 

- **[[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market potential]]** : the maximum saleable quantity of a given product category in a certain geographical area and within a defined time frame; 

- **primary demand** : the quantity actually sold of a certain product category in a specific geographical area and within a defined time frame; 

- **secondary demand** : the quantity sold by a single company; 

- **[[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|potential gap]]** : the difference between actual market sales and hypothetical sales potential; 

- **competitive gap** : the difference between primary demand and the demand satisfied by a single company.

## **Calculating [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market potential]] (1/6)** 

- The potential market represents the maximum saleable quantity of a specific product category in a given market, with reference to a predefined time horizon. 

- It is a _hypothetical_ limit quantity. 

- Primary demand tends asymptotically towards potential. 

- Potential is assumed to be fixed in the short term but subject to change in the long term.

## [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|Potential Gap]] Exercise (consumer goods) 

In 2020, the Italian population stood at around 60 million (59,641,488). Soft drinks are strongly discouraged in the diets of those at the extreme ends of the population, i.e. very young children and elderly adults. Let us assume that the first group consists of children under the age of 5, who in Italy in 2020 represented 3.8% of the Italian population, while the second group consists of adults over the age of 75, who represented 11.7% of the Italian population in 2020. Let us also assume that there are no budget constraints on the purchase of this product category. Furthermore, let us assume that soft drinks are consumed as a cold beverage accompanying meals throughout the day; therefore, we expect an ideal consumption of three times a day and a 33cl bottle on each occasion of consumption. 

_Question 1: Estimate the [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market potential]] for soft drinks in Italy in 2020._ 

Statistical surveys across the country show a significant decrease in consumption in recent years, a trend that began well before the Coronavirus pandemic. In Italy, total actual soft drink consumption in 2020 stood at around 2,628,000,000 liters. Market research shows that 52% of the population does not consume soft drinks. Furthermore, those who do consume soft drinks do so on average only once a day and consume an average of 0.25 cl per occasion. _Question 2: Calculate the [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|potential gap]] (including the non-consumer gap, occasion gap, and dose gap)._

**Non-consumer gap:** 52%–15.5% = 36.5% 

36.5% do not consume soft drinks despite having no objective impediments to doing so – that is, in the context of the example, despite not belonging to the segments of the population for which soft drink consumption is strongly discouraged. Therefore, the quantity of soft drinks not consumed by these individuals is equal to: 

60,000,000 x 36.5% x (3x365) x 0.33 = 7,913,565,000 l 

**Occasion gap:** 48% of Italians consume soft drinks and do so on average only once a day. This means that two consumption occasions are lost per day, therefore: 60,000,000 x 48% x (2x365) x 0.33 = 6,937,920,000 l 

**Dose gap:** Those who consume soft drinks consume less than the optimal quantity, resulting in a reduction in actual soft drink consumption equal to: 

60,000,000 x 48% x (1x365) x (0.33-0.25) = 840,960,000 l

**Estimate of the potential market for flat-screen TVs in Italy over the last 10 years.** 

In 2020, there were approximately 25,700,000 households in Italy. For 2020, an increase of 3% in the number of households is estimated, each with an average propensity to purchase a television of 90%. Households that did not own a television at the beginning of 2020 accounted for approximately 4% of households existing at the beginning of 2020, and it is estimated that the percentage of these households that will purchase a television in 2020 in Italy will be around 5%. It is estimated that the number of televisions owned by households in Italy is 43,100,000 (i.e., on average, households in Italy own more than one television set). Although there is a significant difference in the Italian propensity to replace televisions, sales trends in recent years seem to show a propensity to replace televisions every 5 years.

## **Relative [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market share]]** 

Calculated as the ratio between the [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market share]] of the i-th company and that of its main competitor(s). 

- The denominator of the ratio may correspond to: 

   - the sales of the largest company operating in the market (this is how the share relative to the dominant leader is calculated); 

   - the sales of the top two or three main competitors (relative [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market share]] of the leaders); 

   - the "closest" competitor in terms of size 

- It provides useful information on the competitive distance of the company from the market leader or the company's most direct competitor.

## NAPURA CASE STUDY 

- Dairy cattle farming in Italy is concentrated in the northern regions and represents a very important segment for the feed industry. 

- Approximately 75% of the 40,000 dairy cattle farms are located in Lombardy, Veneto, Piedmont, and Emilia Romagna. The 1,800,000 head of cattle used for milk production can only be fed with feed as a supplement to, and not as a replacement for, fodder. Consequently, the choice of supplementary feed is particularly important. 

- In 2022, a total of 14.5 million tonnes of feed was sold, of which almost 19% (2.7 million) was destined for dairy cattle. The dairy cattle feed industry, which alone has a turnover of around €1.5 billion, is highly concentrated, with the top four companies accounting for approximately 50% of the market. 

- The market leader, Napura, sold 486,000 tonnes of feed in the dairy cattle segment in 2022, achieving a turnover of 18.6% of the market. Its main competitor, Milkalleva, achieved a [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market share]] of 15% by volume. The other two major companies in the sector, La Rocca and Rastelli, achieved 10% and 6% [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|market share]] by volume, respectively. 

- The distribution of feed for dairy cattle is managed by a total of 1,000 distributors, including consortia and retailers. Napura markets its products to farmers through 240 distributors. These distributors, served by the company, brokered a total of 860,000 tonnes of product in 2022. Napura's budget for 2023 shows an increase in sales, forecast at 492,000 tonnes, with total sales in the sector amounting to 2,754,000 tonnes.

_**average weight of customers served**_ : purchases brokered by customers served/number of customers served = 860,000/240   = _**3584 numerical coverage**_ = number of distributors served/total number of distributors = 240/1,000 = **0.24**

**= Old weighted coverage** 

**31.9%.**
