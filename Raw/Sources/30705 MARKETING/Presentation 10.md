**Explanation of content:**

- **Text explanation:** This slide presents the classic distinction between push and pull channel communication strategies.
  - **Push strategy:** The manufacturer focuses marketing efforts (promotions, discounts, personal selling) on channel intermediaries (wholesalers and retailers) to convince them to stock and "push" the product to end consumers.
  - **Pull strategy:** The manufacturer directs marketing efforts (advertising, social media, branding) directly to end consumers, creating high demand so that consumers ask retailers for the product, thereby "pulling" it through the channel.
- **Graph/table explanation:** The omitted picture [406 x 407] represents flowcharts of push and pull mechanics. It typically shows arrows pointing from Manufacturer $\rightarrow$ Intermediary $\rightarrow$ Consumer for a Push strategy, and a direct loop from Manufacturer $\rightarrow$ Consumer, followed by Consumer $\rightarrow$ Retailer demand, for a Pull strategy.
- **Key takeaways from visuals:** A push strategy uses trade incentives to drive supply-side product availability, whereas a pull strategy uses consumer branding to drive demand-side consumer requests.

**What this slide is really saying (1–3 sentences):**
To get products moving, companies either "push" them through the channel by giving retailers financial incentives to stock them, or "pull" them by advertising directly to consumers so they demand the product from retailers.
