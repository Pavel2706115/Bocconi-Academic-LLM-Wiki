## **The [[Wiki/Concepts/Distribution-Channels-and-Trade-Marketing|distribution channel]]** 

The _path_ that a product (good and/or service) follows to move from the producer's sphere of availability to the customer’s sphere of availability. 

_A set of_ ( _interdependent_ ) _entities_ that perform a complex system of functions and activities required to transfer a product - and its title of ownership - from the producer to the final customer

## **The [[Wiki/Concepts/Distribution-Channels-and-Trade-Marketing|indirect channel]]: vertical dimension – ontractual integration** 

- **Proprietary integration** : the industrial company directly controls the intermediation function; 

- **Contractual integration** : based on exclusive distribution agreements or franchising contracts; 

- **Arm's length relationships** (i.e., no vertical integration): 

   - Controlled channel: results from a natural power imbalance between the industry and the distributor (e.g., when dealing with a well-known brand) 

   - ▪ Individualistic channel: emerges from spontaneous matching of supply and demand, where power is more evenly distributed.

## **Trade [[Wiki/Topics/Marketing|marketing]]**

## **The analysis stage** 

## Objective = to analyze in detail: 

- **Current and channels** : assessment of actual and **potential** prospective 

- purchasing behavior of downstream entities; 

- **Commercial sector** : institutional constraints, degree of concentration among purchasing decision-makers, barriers to entry and exit, competitive dynamics, potential new entrants, and the profile of key prospective distributors; 

- **situation and distribution** : evaluation of the 

- **Competitive performance** and its main in terms of current and 

- company competitors potential [[Wiki/Concepts/Distribution-Channels-and-Trade-Marketing|distribution channels]]; 

- **Behavior of distribution** : for **major companies** strategies development, 

- purchasing policies, market approach, and innovation capabilities; 

- **Needs expressed by intermediate customers** ; 

- **Evaluation criteria** for the inclusion of new products in the assortment.

## **Channel partnerships** 

These are difficult to build because they require: 

- **Mutual commitment** , often achieved by creating mutual vulnerability or interdependence; 

- **Mutual trust** . 

Channel partnerships represent a source of competitive advantage and can reshape the dynamics of competition between companies and distribution systems (e.g., Nike and Walmart collaborating through oneoff initiatives to expand the footwear category in large-scale retail).
