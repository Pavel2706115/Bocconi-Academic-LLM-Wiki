## **Finance and Economic Growth** 

- Throughout most of human history, no (per capita) economic growth 

   - Zero-sum game: more people meant lower incomes 

- Not because of the lack of **technological innovations** : windmill, crop-rotations, even new crops from the Americas… 

   - These pushed incomes up but only temporarily 

- Sustained growth per capita only really picked up after 1650. Why?

## **The role of finance** 

- **Finance played a major role** : without financing, difficult to invest in infrastracture, etc 

- Drain of natural and human resources from the colonies helped. But not the only factor: otherwise Spain would have been the richest country of all 

- Finance is a **promise to future payments** (credit comes from Latin _to trust_ ) 

- Trade by Dutch and British merchants required financing (and insurance) 

- But the system can be sustained only with a belief in growth: otherwise credit (with interest) is not possible

## **Importance of Financial Markets** 

- Financial markets help to efficiently allocate capital by transferring funds from people who lack investment opportunities to people who have them. 

- Financial markets also improve the welfare of consumers, allowing them to better time their consumption.

## **Segments of Financial Markets** 

1. Direct Finance 

   - Borrowers raise funds directly by selling financial assets to lenders. These assets are claims to the borrower’s future income from human and nonhuman real assets 

2. Indirect Finance 

   - Borrowers borrow indirectly from lenders via financial intermediaries, which issue financial instruments which are claims on the borrower’s future income from human or nonhuman real assets
