## Monopoly Pricing 

ØMonopolist will choose the price (and quantity) that maximizes its profit (total Revenue – total Cost), given the demand for its product Ø Optimality condition MR=MC 

Ø If marginal revenue exceeded marginal cost the firm would be better off selling more Ø If marginal revenue were less than marginal cost the firm would be better off selling less ØMarginal cost curve applies as usual 

ØBut now firm is not price taker (as in perfect competition): MR ≠ P 

Ø Need to examine the shape of the marginal revenue curve: additional revenue from selling one extra unit of the good

## Monopolist profit maximization 

## Ø **Step 1: Quantity rule** 

ØIdentify positive sales quantities at which MR=MC. If more than one positive sales satisfies the conditions, identify the one that produces the highest profit.

ØCheck whether the most profitable positive sales quantity from Step 1 is greater profit than shutting down. If it is, that is the profit maximizing choice. If not, then selling nothing is the best option Ø TR ≥VariableCosts Ø p≥AVC(q) Ø p≥min AVC

## Welfare Effects of Monopoly Pricing 

ØBy charging a price above marginal cost, the monopolist makes consumers worse off than under perfect competition 

Ø Consumers who buy the product pay more for it 

Ø Some who would have bought it under perfect competition will not buy it at the higher price ØWelfare effects of monopoly pricing: 

Ø Firm gains Ø Consumers lose 

Ø Deadweight loss incurred 

ØDeadweight loss from monopoly pricing is the amount by which aggregate surplus falls short of its maximum possible level, which is attained in a competitive market
