## Principles of Decision making 

Ø **Ranking principle** : a consumer can rank, in order of preference (though possibly with ties), all potentially available consumption bundles 

## Implications 

Ø Preferences are **complete** : between any pair of alternatives, a consumer either prefers one to the other or is indifferent between them. 

ØA consumer is **indifferent** between two alternatives if he likes (or dislikes) them equally 

Ø Preferences are **transitive** : if the consumer prefers one alternative to a second, and prefers the second alternative to a third, then he also prefers the first alternative to the third. 

## Principles of Decision Making 

Ø **The choice principle** : among the available alternatives, the consumer selects the one that he ranks the highest: 

X, Y, Z 

ØIf Z is preferred to X, and ØZ is preferred to Y, then ØZ is the selected alternative 

## Principles of Decision Making 

Ø **The More-is-better Principle** : When one consumption bundle contains more of every good than a second bundle, a consumer prefers the first bundle to the second (free disposal)

## Substitution between goods 

Ø The IC contains information about the rate at which a consumer can substitute one good for another so that she is indifferent between the two bundles 

Ø The rate of substitution between 2 bundles is given by –ΔY/ΔX 

## Marginal rate of substitution between goods 

ØThe rate at which Madeline is willing to substitute small amounts of pizza with yogurt is called **marginal rate of substitution for yoghurt with pizza** ØThe marginal rate of substitution at bundle A equals the (absolute value) of the slope of the line drawn tangent to the indifference curve running through point A. 

## Substitution between goods 

**Marginal Rate of Substitution for X with Y(at a given point)** : The amount of  good Y that a consumer is willing to give up for an extra unit of good X, such that the agent is on the same indifference curve as before. 

## MRS characteristics 

Ø Assume convex indifference curves (preference for variety) 

Ø Within the same indifference curve, as pizza becomes more scarce and yogurt becomes more plentiful, the MRS for yogurt with pizza falls
