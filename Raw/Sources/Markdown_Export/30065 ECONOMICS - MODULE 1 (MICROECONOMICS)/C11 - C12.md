## Competitive markets 

Ø In a perfectly competitive market, buyers and sellers are **price takers** 

## Ø Characteristics of perfectly competitive markets: 

Ø Buyers and sellers face no **transaction costs** 

- Ø Buyers can easily purchase from the seller who offers a product at the best terms 

## Ø Products are **homogeneous** 

   - Ø Price from different sellers must be the same if they both want to make sales 

- Ø There are **many buyers and sellers** , each accounting for a small fraction of the overall demand or supply of the good 

   - Ø A single producer can increase or reduce its supply, without affecting the market price 

   - Ø A single consumer can increase or reduce its demand, without affecting the market price 

## Markets are really perfectly competitive? 

## ØFew markets meet those conditions: 

Ø transactions costs: transport costs (travel or delivery), travelling to another town etc, searching for the best price Ø Products differ, at least a little bit Ø Many market don’t have lots of firms 

ØNevertheless, useful to study perfectly competitive markets for 2 reasons: 

ØMany markets are highly competitive, behave similarly to perfectly competitive markets 

ØUseful benchmark for market efficiency

## Short-Run and Long-Run Supply Curves 

Ø Short-Run and Long-Run supply curves may be different for 2 reasons: 

Ø Fixed vs. variable inputs Ø Different set of firms 

## Ø Market supply curves: 

Ø **Short-run supply** : Add up the short-run supply curves of all _currently active_ firms (they start at AVCmin) 

Ø **Long-run supply** : Add up the long-run supply curves of all _potential_ suppliers (they start at ACmin) 

Ø **Long-run supply with** _**free entry:**_ anyone who wishes to start a firm has free access to the same technology and entry is unrestricted (unlimited number of potential firms) Ø Long-run market supply curve is a horizontal line at ACmin
