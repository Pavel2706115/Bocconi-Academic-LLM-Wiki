## Asymmetric information 

- Ø **asymmetric Information:** one of the 2 parts of a transaction has more information than the other on the exchanged good or service. 

   - Ø Example: a worker on his ability or effort, the owner of an apartment on the quality of the apartment, a manager on the effort he puts in a project 

## Ø If information is not equally shared: inefficiency 

## Ø 2 types of asymmetric information: 

- Ø **Hidden Characteristics** : Things that one side of a transaction knows about itself that the other side would like to know but does not – e.g. Adverse Selection 

- Ø **Hidden Actions** : Actions taken by one side of an economic relationship that the other side cannot observe– e.g. moral hazard 

## Moral hazard 

Ø Economic situation: 

Ø **Principal** delegates a project to an **agent** 

ØThe action chosen by the agent influences but does not totally determine the outcome 

ØPrincipal and agent have different preferences: ØThe principal prefers a high effort by the agent, while the agent prefers to work with lowest effort 

ØUnobservability of actions +difference In preferences give rise to **moral hazard** 

Ø _Moral hazard: agent takes the less costly action that negatively influences the final outcome._ ØInefficiency
