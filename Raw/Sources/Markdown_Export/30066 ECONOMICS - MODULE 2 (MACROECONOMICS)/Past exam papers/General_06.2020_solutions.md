## **Question 3 (5 points)** 

Explain if, and why, you agree, or do not agree, with the following statement _[when answering, assume that, in each time period, the economy is described by a static IS-LM model, with consumption and investment depending on contemporaneous variables only, and that_ _**the central bank chooses the money supply** ]_ : 

“The prices of stocks at time 𝑡𝑡, €𝑄𝑄𝑡𝑡, will unambiguously rise if, from time 𝑡𝑡 onwards, government purchases of goods and services, 𝐺𝐺, go up”. 

## **SKETCH OF ANSWER** 

_The statement is incorrect.  If the central bank chooses the money supply, so that the LM curve is positively sloped, a permanent fiscal expansion that shifts the IS curve to the right leads in every period to a new equilibrium where both income and the interest rate have gone up. Higher income levels lead to higher expected dividends, but higher interest rates lower the present value of any given stream of future expected dividends. It follows that the effect of the permanent increase in_ 𝐺𝐺 _on the prices of stocks at time_ 𝑡𝑡 _will be uncertain._
