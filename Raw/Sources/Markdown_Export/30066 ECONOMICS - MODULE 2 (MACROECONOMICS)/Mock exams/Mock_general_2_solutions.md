## Question 2: Are the following statements true or false? Motivate your answer. 

- a) The standard Solow model predicts that, following a sufficiently large decrease in the depreciation rate, the stock of capital per worker will converge to a strictly positive value in the long run. 

_True. Since the growth rate will converge to zero, K/N will converge to a positive value._ 

- a) The standard Solow growth model predicts that countries starting from a lower level of GDP worker do faster than countries per necessarily grow 

- starting from a higher level. 

_False. This is a possibility not a necessity. What matters is the distance from the country’s steady state._

Question 3: Kripton trades freely goods and services with the rest of the Empire, operating under a flexible exchange rate regime with perfect capital mobility, and its economy system can be described by the standard open-economy IS-LM model. Suppose that both the domestic and foreign price levels are fixed (so that π=π[e] =0), and that the Marshall-Lerner condition is satisfied. The Central Bank implements a contractionary change in its monetary policy, increasing i. Which of the following is the appropriate graph? Choose one and motivate your answer.

_Answer: The domestic Central Bank increases i. The LM shifts upwards. Suddenly, foreign bonds become less attractive for a given domestic interest rate. Question 6: Are the following statements true or false? Motivate your answer. 

a) Quantitative easing does not affect the supply of broad money. 

_False. QE does affect the supply of broad money, as any transaction that buys or sell financial assets from/to the non-financial private sector._ 

a) Under inflation targeting, if the central bank is missing its target, then the actual unemployment rate remains necessarily above the natural rate. 

_False. Not necessarily. This depends on many factors and in particular on how expectations are formed._
