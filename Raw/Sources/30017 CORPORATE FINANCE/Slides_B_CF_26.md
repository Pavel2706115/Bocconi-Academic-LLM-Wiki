# Corporate Finance 30017 - Slides B

## Risk and Return
*   **Expected Returns:** Probability-weighted average of possible returns.
*   **Variance and Standard Deviation:** Measures of risk (dispersion of returns).
*   **Covariance and Correlation:** Measures of how two assets move together. Correlation ranges from -1 to +1.

## Portfolios
*   Investors hold portfolios to diversify risk.
*   **Idiosyncratic (Unique) Risk:** Firm-specific risk that can be diversified away (e.g., weather risk for an umbrella company).
*   **Systematic (Market) Risk:** Economy-wide risk that affects all stocks and cannot be diversified.
*   The variance of a portfolio depends heavily on the covariance between its assets. As the number of stocks increases, portfolio variance approaches the average covariance (systematic risk).

## The Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)
*   **Beta ($\beta$):** The measure of a stock's systematic risk. It measures the sensitivity of a stock's return to the market portfolio's return. $\beta = \frac{Cov(R_i, R_m)}{Var(R_m)}$
*   **Efficient Frontier:** The set of optimal portfolios that offer the highest expected return for a defined level of risk.
*   **Capital Market Line (CML):** The line from the risk-free rate tangent to the efficient frontier. The tangency point is the Market Portfolio.
*   **Security Market Line (SML):** The graphical representation of the CAPM. It shows the expected return of an individual asset as a function of its Beta.
    *   $E(R_i) = R_f + \beta_i(E(R_m) - R_f)$
*   In equilibrium, all assets should lie exactly on the SML.

## Market Efficiency and Event Studies
*   **Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH):**
    *   *Weak Form:* Prices reflect past prices.
    *   *Semi-Strong Form:* Prices reflect all publicly available information.
    *   *Strong Form:* Prices reflect all information (public and private).
*   **Event Studies:** A methodology to measure the economic impact of an event by observing stock prices. 
    *   *Abnormal Return (AR):* The difference between the actual return and the expected return (usually estimated using the CAPM).
    *   *Cumulative Abnormal Return (CAR):* The sum of ARs over an event window.

## Cost of Capital
*   **Company Cost of Capital (CCC):** The opportunity cost of capital for the firm's average risk asset. If the firm is unlevered, CCC = Cost of Equity.
*   A project should only be evaluated using the CCC if it has the same risk as the firm's existing average assets. Otherwise, a project-specific discount rate (e.g., using pure-play comparables) must be used.
*   **Determinants of Asset Beta:** Cyclicality (sensitivity to the business cycle) and Operating Leverage (ratio of fixed to variable costs).
