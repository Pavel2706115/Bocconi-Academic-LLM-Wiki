A family chooses how many units of education E to purchase for their child. The family’s marginal willingness to pay for education is MWP = 30-2E. The marginal cost of education is constant and equal to MC = 6.  
a) Compute the privately optimal level of education.  
b) Suppose education also generates a positive externality of 4 per unit for society through higher  
productivity and civic participation. Compute the socially optimal level of education.  
c) Does the private market lead to underinvestment or overinvestment in education?  
d) Suppose the government provides 5 units of education for free, while families can purchase  
additional education at the market price. How much education will the family consume in total? How much education will it purchase privately?  
e) Does public provision move education closer to the social optimum? Explain briefly.

Solution:
a) 
$MWP = 30 - 2 \times E$
$MC = 6$
$30 - 2 \times E = 6$
$E = 12 \ \text{private}$

b)
$SMB = 30 - 2 \times E + 4$
$SMB = MC$
$30 - 2 \times E + 4 = 6$
$E = 14 \ \text{socially optimal}$

The private market leads to underinvestment in education.

2:
Given:
Consider an individual who worked and paid contributions for four periods. Her wages in the first 
three periods were:  
• Period 1: 12  
• Period 2: 18  
• Period 3: 24  
The pension is computed using the earnings-based method, with the pensionable wage equal to the  
average wage over the full career. The coefficient of return is β = 0,05. It is observed that the pension  
received by the individual is equal to 4.  
a) Compute the wage earned by the individual in the fourth period.  
b) Briefly explain how the pension would change if, instead, the pensionable wage was equal to the last wage earned.

Solution:
a)
$P=\beta \times w_p \times L$
$\beta = 0.05$
$L = 4$
$w_p = \frac{12 + 18 + 24 + x}{4}$
$P = 4$
$4 = 0.05 \times w_p \times 4$
$w_p = 20$
$20 = \frac{12 + 18 + 24 + x}{4}$
=>$x = 26$

b)
$P = 0.05 \times 26 \times 4$
P = 5.2
The pension would increase by 1.2, if instead, the pensionable wage was equal to the last wage earned - 26.
