## CHANGES IN NOMINAL GDP

- Nominal GDP changes for two reasons:

– Changes in the production

- Changes in prices

If our goal is to measure production and its change over time, we need to eliminate the effect of changes in prices (i.e. inflation/deflation)

_How can we?_

_Necessary to construct another indicator that changes only if quantities change: REAL GDP_

## REAL GDP

- We choose a reference year (called base year) and we compute the GDP using always the prices of that year

Real GDP (at constant prices) =  value of current production based on prices of a year chosen as reference (base year, b):

Yt= p1bQ1t + p2bQ2t +…

- It changes only if the produced quantity changes (different economy size) => stats cleaned from the price dynamics

- Choice of b is arbitrary

- When b is changed, figures must be updated the figures must be updated accordingly

## INFLATION (PRICE DYNAMICS)

## THE PRICE DYNAMICS

- In the economy prices _react_ to changes in the D and/or S

- Prices can be o _**flexible sticky**_

- _How do we call their variation?_

**Inflation:** a sustained rise in the general level of prices

**Deflation**: a sustained decline in the general level  of prices(negative inflation)

## THE INFLATION RATE

– The rate at which the price level changes  =>   % change in the general level of prices between two periods

– _What prices do we consider?_

**①Prices of the production** (not necessarily consumed) – GDP deflator– **P t**

**②Prices of consumed goods and services** (cost of living)

– Consumer Price Index – CPI t

## ①PRODUCTION PRICES

- IDEA: If nominal GDP increases faster that the real GDP, the difference must come from an increase in prices

- GDP deflator = Ratio btw nominal and real GDP in a given year

- It measures the  average price  of the final goods and services produced in the economy

- Index number equal to 1 in the base year

- Its rate of change  (%) represents the rate of inflation of the production

## IS THE GDP DEFLATOR A GOOD PRICE INDEX?

It depends on the purpose since…

- Not all produced goods go to final national consumers

- Some produced goods are

- Not sold domestically ( _exports_ )

- Not sold to final consumers (but to firms or to the government)

- Not all consumed goods are produced by domestic firms

- Some of the goods bought by consumers produced abroad ( _imports_ )

## ②COST OF LIVING

- CONSUMER PRICE INDEX = it measures in the national currency (€, $,…) the cost of a basket of goods and services consumed by the typical urban consumer

- It measures the  average price  of the goods and services consumed in the economy

- Prices are weighted according to the purchase frequency

- It accounts for changes in the cost of living and the purchasing power of wages

- Computed monthly

- Inflation rate based in the CPI = % change in the CPI

- Monetary Policy in EU is set using the Harmonised Index of Consumer Price (HICP)

- Hyperinflation: extremely high % change in the CPI (100% in 24h)

Same procedure as before but considering consumed goods irrespective from where they are produced

1. Identify the consumer basket

2. Compute the CPI

CPI = Ratio btw nominal and real value of the consumed basket in a given year

3. Compute its rate of change  (%) that is  the rate of inflation of the consumed goods
