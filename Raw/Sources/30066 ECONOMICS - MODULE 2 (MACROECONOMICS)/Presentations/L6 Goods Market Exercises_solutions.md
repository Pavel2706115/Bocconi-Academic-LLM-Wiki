## GRAPHICAL ANALYSIS

## INTUITION

When c0 changes, Y changes because of two mechanisms

- **Direct effect**: cc0 ↓⇒ZZ↓⇒YY↓

- The exogenous reduction of consumption reduces demand, hence production and income

- **Indirect effect:** ⇒YY↓⇒CC↓⇒ZZ↓⇒YY↓⇒CC↓⇒ZZ↓… . ⇒ZZ↓↓

- The reduction in income leads to a further decline in consumption and demand, reducing production and income more than proportionally

## IMPACT ON CONSUMPTION

Quantify the change in C starting from its equation by applying the Δ to both members and remembering that cc0 changes **but, in equilibrium, also Y changes**

C decreases fro two reasons: 1. cc0 ↓⇒CC↓ 2. in equilibrium YY↓, CC↓

- Consumption declines more than proportionally compared to the initial exogenous reduction

- The decline in consumption is exactly equal to the decline in income
