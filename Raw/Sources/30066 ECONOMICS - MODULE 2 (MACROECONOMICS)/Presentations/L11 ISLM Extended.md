# REAL VS NOMINAL INTEREST RATE

Distinguish btw the two is relevant if there is inflation in the economy

_**Problem:**_ Suppose you want a coffee that costs 1€ but you don’t have any money with you. What should you do?

_Ask for a loan to a friend!_

_**What you can do in the future?**_

_**1) Pay your debt with money**_

How much you will have to _pay back_ in the future depends on _**nominal interest rate**_

_**2) Pay your debt with coffees**_

How many coffees you have to give to your friend depend on today and tomorrow **prices** and the **nominal interest rate**

Same reasoning if today you do not take a coffee today

⇒ You save 1€

⇒ By investing this 1€, in one year from now you will have 1,1€ (nominal return)

⇒ In the next period you can take 1,1/2=0,5 coffee more (real return)

## RELATION BTW REAL AND NOMINAL INTEREST RATE

If at time t we want to consume 1 unit more (less) of goods, we need PPtt euros more (less)

**Nominal interest rate (i** ~~**t**~~ **):** Amount of **euro** we must pay in the future to get an additional **euro** today

If at t we save (borrow) 1€, at t+1 we get (have to pay) (1+it)€

**Real interest rate (r** ~~**t**~~ **):** Amount of **goods** we must give in the future in exchange of a **good** today

If at t we save (borrow) the amount necessary to by 1 unit of goods that costs Pt, at t+1 we get (have to pay) the equivalent of (1+rt) units of good that will cost Pt+1
*(See also: [[Trigari_05(Chapter 6)#^lb20qc]])*

The two interest rates are linked through the dynamic of prices btw t and t+1

We can simplify this expression to better understand the link using the inflation rate concept in a given period

…But, as future prices are not known but simply expected, we have the **expected inflation rate**:

## Arbitrage condition in financial markets:

_In order to exchange both risky and risk – free assets the expected return of the risky asset must be equal to the return paid by the risk-free asset_

– If 1 + ii> 1 −pp 1 + ii+ xx⇒ Investors buy risk-free assets only

– If 1 + ii< 1 −pp 1 + ii+ xx⇒ Investors buy risky assets only

- Both assets are exchanged if:

1 + ii= 1 −pp 1 + ii+ xx
