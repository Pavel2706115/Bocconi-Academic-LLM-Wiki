General Exam 2

Time: 90 minutes   |   Closed book   |   Answer all questions

Q1. Yield curve with 1Y and 2Y bonds (expectations hypothesis)
Inflation is zero today and expected to be zero next year. At time t: the current 1‑year yield is i1_t = 2.5% and the expected 1‑year yield next year is i1_{t+1}^e = 4.5%.
(a) Compute the 2‑year yield today i2_t under the simple expectations hypothesis.
(b) Now markets expect tighter policy next year (i1_{t+1}^e rises further). What happens to i2_t and to the slope of the curve? Explain with IS–LM intuition for next year affecting today via expectations.

Q2. Policy rule difference and current slope
Country Beta is like Q1 but its CB fixes M (positively sloped LM). Explain why an anticipated monetary tightening at t+1 can lower today’s short rate i1_t when the CB fixes M, while raising i2_t, and why the curve typically gets steeper. Provide a clean intuition using IS feedback from lower Y_t to money demand.

Q3. Open economy with fixed credible E
Delta runs a fixed, credible exchange rate: E = E^e = ar E. UIP then implies i = i*. Starting from equilibrium, Δc0 = ΔT̄ > 0 (autonomous consumption and net taxes rise by the same amount).
(a) Which curve(s) move? Explain effects on Y, C, I, NX, i, E.
(b) What must the CB do with M to keep E fixed along the transition? Explain money market logic.

Q4. Liquidity trap and fiscal policy
Suppose the economy is at the ZLB (i = 0, flat LM). Consider a rise in Ḡ financed by debt.
(a) Show the effect on Y and i in IS–LM. 
(b) Discuss crowding‑out vs crowding‑in at the ZLB and the role of expected future policy for multipliers.
(c) Name two conditions under which fiscal multipliers are likely >1.

Q5. National saving identity: simultaneous Δc0 and ΔT̄
In the goods‑market model with Y = C + I + G, C = c0 + c1(Y − T), I = Ī, G = Ḡ, T = T̄, analyze the effect of Δc0 = ΔT̄ > 0 on Y, private saving S^priv, public saving S^pub, and national saving S^nat. Provide sign results and intuition (no need for full algebra).
