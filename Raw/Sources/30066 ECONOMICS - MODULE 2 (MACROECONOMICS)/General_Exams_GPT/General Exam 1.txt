General Exam 1

Time: 90 minutes   |   Closed book   |   Answer all questions

Q1. IS–LM coordination (closed economy, prices fixed)
In Macro-land, I = Ī (exogenous), C = c0 + c1(Y − T), G = Ḡ, T = T̄. The central bank sets i.
Starting from equilibrium, the government raises Ḡ and T̄ by the same amount (ΔḠ = ΔT̄ > 0).
(a) Show the effect on IS and on Y if i is kept at its initial target.
(b) If the central bank wants to keep Y at its initial level, what open market operation is needed? Explain using the money market diagram and bond pricing logic.
(c) How does the composition of demand (C, I, G) change between the initial and final equilibria?

Q2. Private saving under fiscal–monetary mix
Same economy as Q1. Derive the private saving function S^priv = Y − T − C. Evaluate how S^priv changes between the initial equilibrium and the final equilibrium of Q1 when the central bank offsets to keep Y constant.

Q3. Money market mechanics: choosing i vs choosing M
Two countries, Alfa and Beta, are identical except policy: Alfa’s CB chooses i; Beta’s CB fixes nominal money supply M at M̄. Individuals hold money as currency (c fraction) and deposits; banks hold a reserve ratio θ. From an initial common equilibrium, c increases permanently.
(a) What happens to the money multiplier and to M given base H if the CB does nothing?
(b) Explain the CB reaction needed in each regime (Alfa, Beta) to keep the same market outcome as initially (same i in Alfa; same M in Beta).
(c) Conclude whether i changes in either country.

Q4. Medium-run natural rate and policy mix (IS–LM–PC with flexible prices)
In WS–PS, a fall in firms’ markup m raises Yn. At the same time, net taxes T̄ rise permanently.
(a) Track the effects on the IS–LM–PC diagram: which curves shift and why?
(b) Define the natural real interest rate r* (Wicksellian). Compared to the initial medium-run equilibrium, does r* rise, fall, or stay the same? Explain the intuition.
(c) Compare the composition of demand at the new medium run equilibrium to the initial one (C, I, G).

Q5. Debt dynamics (numbers)
Assume g = 1% (0.01) and real interest rate r = 6% (0.06). At time t, the debt ratio bt = B_t / Y_t = 1.20 (120%) and the primary balance-to-GDP is pb_t = (T_t − G_t^prim)/Y_t = −0.03 (a 3% primary deficit).
(a) Compute b_{t+1} from b_{t+1} = (1 + r − g) b_t − pb_t.
(b) Find the growth rate ĝ that stabilizes b at 120% next period for the given r and pb_t.
(c) Give one fiscal and one growth policy lever that could achieve stabilization in practice and discuss trade‑offs briefly.
