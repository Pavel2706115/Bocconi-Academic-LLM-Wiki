General Exam 3

Time: 90 minutes   |   Closed book   |   Answer all questions

Q1. Extended IS–LM with risk premium
Spending depends on the borrowing rate r + x. From an initial equilibrium, x rises and Ḡ falls. The CB adjusts r to keep Y unchanged. What happens to the borrowing rate r + x in the new equilibrium? Explain with IS logic.

Q2. Debt steady state and sustainability
(a) Write the steady‑state debt ratio b* given constant primary balance pb and constant g, r. 
(b) Suppose pb = −0.02 (2% deficit), r = 0.03, g = 0.015. Compute b* (if defined). Comment on sustainability and what must change to stabilize at b* ≤ 60%.
(c) Briefly explain the “snowball” effect (r − g) on debt dynamics.

Q3. UIP calculation
Uncovered interest parity: (1 + i) = (1 + i*) (E^e_{t+1}/E_t). Given i = 3%, i* = 5%, and E^e_{t+1} = 1.30 $/€ (expected in one year), compute today’s E_t and state whether the euro is expected to appreciate or depreciate.

Q4. Marshall–Lerner, J‑curve (goods‑only, fixed P)
(a) State the Marshall–Lerner condition and the J‑curve intuition.
(b) Under ML holding, how does a real depreciation affect NX on impact vs over time? Explain with price vs quantity effects and existing contracts.

Q5. Two‑period taxes and Ricardian equivalence
In a 2‑period model with access to credit at real rate r, the government cuts current taxes by 100 and announces +100 next period (per capita), with r = 0.
(a) For an unconstrained consumer with zero initial wealth, derive ΔC_t and ΔC_{t+1}. What happens to private saving at t?
(b) How do results change if some consumers are liquidity constrained? Provide intuition for aggregate demand effects today.
