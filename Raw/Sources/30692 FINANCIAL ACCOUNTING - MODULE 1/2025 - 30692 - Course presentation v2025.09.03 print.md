## Assessment methods (intro)

_Premise._ We strongly believe in the importance of a process of **continuous learning.** Therefore, in a nutshell, during the semester the following occurs:

1. During the lectures in class, you will receive a number of questions (the so-called “ **In Class Questions”, “ICQ”** ) for a total of max. 4 points; however, only the first **3 points** will be counted. Any fraction of point above 3 will remain unused.

2. On December 1(st) at 8 AM, when you are at home, you will receive in Blackboard a Quiz that totals max. 3 points and so you may get a total of max. **3 points** as **“Bonus Quiz at home”** .

We believe that the Intended Learning Outcomes can be achieved at the top level ONLY if you actively follow the lectures and continuously study the materials provided. Nevertheless, **you are absolutely free to decide to skip** all these activities and projects.

Then, **when you register to sit for the exam** , you have two alternative options…

## Written exam

Students **must register** to sit for the exam. A student not registered is not allowed to take the exam.

At the end of the term, all students have a written exam. **A part of the exam text will be provided on paper but answers shall be entered on the student’s laptop** (i.e., no paper), administered through Blackboard Original (i.e., NO Blackboard Ultra) and will use the software Lockdown Browser. The question types can be basically grouped as follows: ^qxufm0

- Closed ended questions (Multiple Choice, “true or false” and/or Multiple Answer questions) which (in the “NO BONUS” exam) are worth approx. 09-13 points.

- -

- Open ended questions (journal entries or other types of open-ended questions, e.g. exercises, fill in the blank etc) which (in the “NO BONUS” exam) are worth approx. 18-22 points.

The time available for the No Bonus exam will be more than that for the Yes Bonus exam. Grades might be curved upwards.

## In-class questions

During the semester, in each class, all professors will be entitled to assign points at their complete discretion, asking random questions ( **ICQ** , In Class Questions) anytime during the lesson on the platform Blackboard Ultra at their convenience (the release date and time of the ICQ are not predetermined).

Also if you are in class with your classmates when the questions are released, you shall answer the ICQ individually ( _i.e., you can’t talk with anyone during the time available_ ); though, the assessment is open-book ( _i.e., during the time available to answer, you can check your notes, textbook, etc._ ).

Broadly speaking, the professors will release 10 to 12 ICQ, each worth 0,3 / 0,4 points during the semester. The total points students may earn with ICQ over the semester is up to 4 (FOUR).

Then, if you register to sit for the Yes Bonus exam, up to 3 points out of 4 from the In-Class Questions points will be automatically added to the exam points. Any fraction of point in excess to 3 and up to 4 will remain unused.

3 points out of 4 is the equivalent of 75% attendance, but in this case we don’t award a “passive approach” (I am physically in class so I am attending) but instead an active participation (I follow the lectures and I am awarded for answering simple questions on topics explained by the professor).

## Bonus Quiz at home

One short is to the students via Blackboard the course. This quiz given during quiz may consist of closed-ended and/or open-ended questions. It lasts approximately 10 minutes, and its main goal is to foster continuous assessment.

The quiz is not in presence. The quiz does not require the use of Lockdown Browser.

The release date of the quiz is scheduled on the following date: **December 01 at 08.00 AM Italian time** and covers up to chapter 9 (included) of the book (other details will be provided in a separate file) (Chapter 10 and the “advanced topics” are not assessed in the quiz).

If the student decides to register for the 25-point exam and not for the 31-point exam, the (up to) 6 points of the Quiz and ICQ are automatically assigned. In other words, these points are not “in addition to” the grade of the exam, but rather, these points are “SUBSTITUTIVE” of exam exercises that would be worth 6 points. In any possible scenario (absence, failure, withdrawal) occurring in one exam session, the points scored in the Quiz and ICQ are still valid. They may be used in the subsequent sessions up to June 24th (included).

## Attendance

Students ' attendance is recorded through the “Attendance” procedure.

NOTE: there is NO RELATION between in-class questions and the “Attendance” procedure. “Attendance” is recorded only for statistical purposes and gives NO extra points. There are no differences between the assessment and grading methods for “attending” and “not attending” students.

Obviously, the more a student attends and actively follows the lessons, the more ICQ points will (likely) score. Nevertheless, a student might also attend 1 lesson out of 22 (thus being “notattending” for Attendance purposes) and get (for example) 0,4 points because he/she was there on the day in which an ICQ was released; conversely, an “attending” student (22 lectures out of 22) might get zero ICQ points if he/she answers all the questions wrong during the semester. ^cvkpjv
