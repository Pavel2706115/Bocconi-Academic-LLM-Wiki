## Utility 

Ø Utility is a numeric value indicating the consumer’s **relative** well being from consuming a bundle of goods. Ø Higher utility indicates greater satisfaction than lower utility. 

Ø A **utility function** is a mathematical formula that assigns a utility value to each consumption bundle: U(X,Y)=f(X,Y) 

Ø Example: U(F,C)=2F+5(FxC) 

A basket of Fries=2 and Chicken=1 gives a utility: U(2,1)=2x2+5(2x1)=14 

## Ordinal vs. Cardinal Utility 

Ø **Ordinal information** allows us to determine only whether one alternative is better or worse than another. 

Ø **Cardinal information** tells us something about the _intensity_ of those preferences. 

Ø In modern microeconomic theory, utility functions are only intended to summarize ordinal information, rejecting old cardinal interpretations of utility. Ø Example: if one consumption bundle has a utility value of 10 and a second has a utility value of 5, we know the consumer prefers the first to the second, but it does not necessarily make him twice as happy.
