## Elasticity of Demand

- - We are interested in measuring the percentage change in the individual demand for a good for each 1% change in:

1. The price of the good

2. The price of other goods 3. The consumer’s income

- Measures how responsive the quantity demanded is to small changes in prices " %$%&'() +' , = - # ! %$%&'() +' !

- - Expect E(d) to be negative:

`o` When P increases, amount demanded typically decreases

- When P decreases, amount demanded typically increases

## Categories of Elasticity of Demand

- - ELASTIC: IE(d) I>1

`o` Meaning: 1% rise in price reduces consumption by more than 1%.

- - INELASTIC: I E(d) I < 1

`o` Meaning: a 1% rise in price reduces consumption by less than 1%

- - UNIT ELASTIC: IE(d) I= 1

`o` Meaning: a 1% rise in price reduces consumption by 1%.

## Categories of Elasticity of Demand

## Extreme cases:

- PERFECTLY ELASTIC: E(d ) = - ∞ (horizontal D curve) `o` Meaning: a rise in the price of the good induces consumers to stop purchasing it. - PERFECTLY INELASTIC:   E(d) = 0 (vertical D curve) `o` Meaning: a rise in the price of the good won’t affect consumption.

## Price Elasticity of Demand

- Goods tend to have more price elastic demand when:

`o` → They have close substitutes if price changes, consumers can easily switch to an alternative product

- Buyers of the product consider it a luxury

- Buyers of the product have low income and thus are sensitive to changes in their expenditures

- - _In general_ , elasticity of demand varies at different points along a demand curve

- - At very high prices a 2% increase will have different effect on demand than at very low prices

- we measure elasticity separately at each point of the demand curve (i.e. at each initial price)

## Total Expenditure and Elasticity of Demand

- - _**Total expenditure (TE)**_ =PxQ, the product of the price and the total amount demanded - **%change in TE=%change in Q+ %change in P**

- - Elasticity of demand tells us how total expenditure changes when price decreases

- - **TE** will increase with a small decrease in price when demand is elastic and decrease when demand is inelastic

- - If E **(d)** =-1, then a 1% increase in P will decrease Q by exactly 1%, leaving **TE** unchanged.

- - TE is largest at a price for which elasticity equals -1

## Price, Elasticity, and Total Expenditure
