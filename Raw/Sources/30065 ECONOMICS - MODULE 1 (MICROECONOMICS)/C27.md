ØRisky choices often have financial consequences, **payoffs** ØPayoffs can be positive (gains) or negative (losses) 

ØEvery outcome has an associate payoff. Since every outcome happens with some probability, that probability is also the probability of the associate payoff.

## Ø An _**expected utility function:**_ 

Ø Assigns a utility level to each payoff resulting from a possible outcome 

Ø Then take the expected value of those utilities Ø It is a weighted average of all possible utility levels using the probability of each level as its weight

## Certainty equivalent 

Ø The _**certainty equivalent**_ of a risky bundle is the amount of money which, if provided with certainty, would make the consumer equally well off: U(CE)=E(U(lottery)) 

## Risk premium 

ØThe _**risk premium**_ of a risky bundle is the difference between its expected value and the ’ consumer s certainty equivalent: risk premium=(EV-CE) ØAmount by which a consumer is willing to reduce expected consumption to eliminate all risk
