## Price discrimination 

- Ø Monopolist might be able to do better by _**price discriminating:**_ charging different prices for different units of the same good 

- Ø To be able to price discriminate: 

   1. A firm must have some market power Ø If not, a price above marginal cost will result in zero sales 

   2. The good or service must be difficult to resell Ø Otherwise few sales will occur at the higher price 

   3. Firm must also be able to distinguish sales for which the purchasers have a high willingness to pay from those they have a low willingness to pay: 

Ø Perfect price discrimination

## Perfect price discrimination 

A monopolist can engage in **perfect price discrimination** if: 

Ø He knows a customer’s willingness to pay for each unit he sells 

Ø He can charge a different price for each unit 

èHe will charge each customer exactly his willingness to pay for the good! Pi=WTPi èHe will sell all the units for which willingness to pay (marginal revenue) is higher (or equal) than marginal cost: he will stop at P=MR=MC!

Suppose inverse market demand is given by P=50-Q and that the monopolist can perfectly price discriminate among consumers. Assume moreover it is illegal to resell units of the good. Monopolist total costs are given by TC=25+3Q. What is the Q that the monopolist will optimally sell?  What about consumer surplus, DWL and monopolist profits? 

## Solution 

Ø Set P=MC to find the quantity that the monopolist will sell: 50-Q=3èQ[M] =47, the monopolist will set prices ranging from P=50 to P=3. 

Ø CS=0 

Ø DWL=0 Ø PS=(50-3)47/2=1104.5 Ø Monopolist profits Ø SURPLUS-FIXED COSTS : Ø 1104.5-25=1079.5 Ø (or TR-TC) 

Ø TR=area of trapezoid: 0.5(50+3)47=1245.5 Ø TC=25+3x47=166 

Ø Profits=1245.5-166=1079.5 

## Two-Part Tariffs 

Ø In practice, it is very difficult to implement perfect price discrimination based on each consumer’s WTP for every different level of quantity demanded 

Ø Two-part tariffs are another (simpler) pricing plan that allows a perfectly discriminating monopolist to maximize profit 

Ø With a _**two-part tariff**_ , consumers pay an initial fixed fee plus a separate per-unit price for each unit they buy Ø Examples: amusement parks, rental car companies, nightclubs Ø Advantage is simplicity: name just two prices for each buyer Ø To maximize profit: 

Ø set per-unit price equal to marginal cost Ø Fixed fee=CS left after the per unit price

## Observable Customer Characteristics 

Ø Most often a firm’s ability to price discriminate is imperfect Ø May be able to sort consumers into rough groups based on **observable characteristics** : a firm can distinguish, even if imperfectly, consumers with a high versus low willingness to pay 

   - Ø Example: small town movie theater with four consumer groups (adults, seniors, students, kids) 

- Ø To maximize profit consider each group’s demand curve separately 

   - Ø Set price to maximize profit earned from that group (MR=MC for each group)è find different prices for different groups! 

## Observable Customer Characteristics 

Ø Charge a higher price to groups with less elastic demand: − _P MC_ = −[1] _D P E_ Ø Profits higher than without price discrimination (but not as high as with perfect price discrimination) Ø DWL>0 (inefficiency) Ø CS>0
