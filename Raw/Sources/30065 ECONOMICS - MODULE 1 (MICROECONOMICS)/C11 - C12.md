# Econ 30065: Microeconomics Sara Negrelli

Lecture 15: Competitive Market Equilibrium

## Competitive markets

- In a perfectly competitive market, buyers and sellers are **price takers**

- Characteristics of perfectly competitive markets:

- Buyers and sellers face no **transaction costs**

- - Buyers can easily purchase from the seller who offers a product at the best terms

- Products are **homogeneous**

- - Price from different sellers must be the same if they both want to make sales

- - There are **many buyers and sellers** , each accounting for a small fraction of the overall demand or supply of the good

- - A single producer can increase or reduce its supply, without affecting the market price

- - A single consumer can increase or reduce its demand, without affecting the market price

## Markets are really perfectly competitive?

- Few markets meet those conditions:

- transactions costs: transport costs (travel or delivery), travelling to another town etc, searching for the best price - Products differ, at least a little bit - Many market don’t have lots of firms

- Nevertheless, useful to study perfectly competitive markets for 2 reasons:

- Many markets are highly competitive, behave similarly to perfectly competitive markets

- Useful benchmark for market efficiency

## Market Demand

- **Market demand (at a given price):** sum of the demands of all the individual consumers

- **Market demand curve:** horizontal sum of the individual demand curves

## Market Supply

- **Market supply (at a given price):** sum of the supply of all the individual sellers

- **Market supply curve:** horizontal sum of the individual supply curves

## Competitive market equilibrium

- At equilibrium price, Qs=Qd

=>Market clears

- Given demand and supply functions, use algebra to find the equilibrium

## Short-Run and Long-Run Supply Curves

- Short-Run and Long-Run supply curves may be different for 2 reasons:

- Fixed vs. variable inputs - Different set of firms

- Market supply curves:

- **Short-run supply**: Add up the short-run supply curves of all _currently active_ firms (they start at AVCmin)

- **Long-run supply**: Add up the long-run supply curves of all _potential_ suppliers (they start at ACmin)

- **Long-run supply with** _**free entry:**_ anyone who wishes to start a firm has free access to the same technology and entry is unrestricted (unlimited number of potential firms) - Long-run market supply curve is a horizontal line at ACmin

## The Long -Run Market Supply Curve with Free Entry

In the LR, firms in a perfectly competitive market MUST operate at their efficient scale of production (where $$AC=MC)$$

## Long-Run Competitive Equilibrium with Free Entry

## -: **Properties**

- Firms earn zero profit

- Otherwise if positive profits new firms would enter

- If negative profits some firms would exit

- Until P=ACmin - Equilibrium price equals ACmin - Each active firm produces at its efficient scale of production

## Consumer Surplus

- **Consumer surplus**: net benefit that a consumer receives from participating in the market for some good:

**Sum of (how much he is willing to pay each unit of a good – price he actually pays for the good)** - Demand tells us how much he is willing to pay each unit of a good

- Market price tells us how much he actually pays for the good

## Economic Efficiency

- **Pareto efficient** (or **Pareto optimal** ): an economic outcome at which it is impossible to make anyone better off without making someone else worse off

At the efficient outcome the aggregate surplus is maximized

## Perfectly competitive markets and efficiency

- Perfectly competitive market produces an outcome that is economically efficient

## Deadweight Loss

- **Deadweight Loss:** a reduction in aggregate surplus below its maximum possible value
