# Econ 30065: Microeconomics Sara Negrelli

Class 29: Adverse Selection and the Job market

## Interventions to improve efficiency

- Warranties and reputations – private solutions - Warranty – seller guarantees to take back the good if consumer is not satisfied (often for durable goods) **SIGNALLING**

- Reputations – sellers try to build reputation over time that they have tested the good they sell and their quality is maintained over time/space

- - Public interventions

- Licenses (e.g. for doctors, health centers, pharmacies) - Mandatory checks to ensure quality and safety standards are maintained

- - Remedies solve the moral hazard problem but still inefficiency

## Example with warranty

Assume in the example of the cars with MCG=4 and MCB=2 it is possible to offer a warranty. Suppose the warranty costs 0.1 for owners of good car and 2.3 for owners of bad cars. Then the warranty solves the adverse selection problem. Pw=4.4 (profits 4.4-4-0.1=0.3>0) PNW=2.2 (profits 2.2-2=0.2>4.4 -2-2.3=0.1)

## Example: signalling on the job market

- Things might change if type H could **signal** her ability

- But just saying “I am type H" is not a credible signal, as type L could easily say the same

- - Signal must be _credible_

- A signal is credible if sending it is convenient for type H, but not for type L - In reality, higher education-costly action- serves (also) this purpose - Indeed, besides higher productivity, higher ability also implies easier achievement of higher education levels (i.e less costly to get it for an H type!) - How do conclusions change when signalling through education is possible?
*(See also: [[L4 L5 Education#^hxxi5d]])*

## Example: signalling

- Focus on case where worker proposes wage

- Before proposal is made, worker chooses whether to obtain a college degree

- Obtaining degree entails a disutility to worker (costly action) - H L The disutility is decreasing in ability: C for type H and C for type L, with CL > CH

- Assume for simplicity that education does not affect productivity - It works only as a signal!

## Example: signalling

- Ability still unobservable to firm

- But firm does observe worker's education level

→ Firm can condition decision to accept/reject worker's proposal on her education level

- - We are interested in _separation_: this means type H goes to college but type L does not

- If separation happens, firm infers ability from education èpay up to 100 for graduate

- Type H proposes 100, firm accepts

## Example: signalling

- When does separation occur?

- We need 2 constraint - Type H must find it preferable to graduate: 100-C ≥55 H - Type L must find it preferable NOT to graduate: 100-C <55 L èSeparation occurs if and only if:

C >45≥C L H

## Example: signalling

- When signaling opportunities are available and separation happens, highly productive worker behaves differently from less productive one - Only type H chooses higher level of education - While ability remains not observable directly, by observing worker's education choice in pre-contractual phase firm indirectly but perfectly infers worker's ability

- This is why firm accepts type H worker's proposal of 100!

## signalling

- - Signalling not as efficient as symmetric information because now the social cost is higher (55+C)→ total surplus is lower.

- Note that this is only true because we assumed education and hard tasks not to have any real (productivity) value - If it were the other way, effect on total surplus would be _ambiguous_
