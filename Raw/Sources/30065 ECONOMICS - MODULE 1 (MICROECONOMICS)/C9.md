## Production Technologies

- **Inputs**: the materials, labor, land, or equipment that firms use to produce their outputs

- **Outputs**: the physical products or services a firm produces (Q)

- **Production function**: states mathematically the amount of output a firm can produce from given amounts of inputs using efficient production methods: Q=F(inputs)

## Production

- Most production processes use many variable inputs: labor, capital, materials, and land - We assume two homogeneous inputs: labor (L) and capital (K)

- Capital inputs include assets such as physical plant, machinery, and vehicles - Production function:

= _F L K Q_ ( , )

## Production in the Short Run and the Long Run

- - **Variable input**: can be adjusted over the time period being considered

- - **Fixed input**: cannot be adjusted over the time period being considered

- - **Short run**: a period of time over which one or more inputs is fixed

- - **Long run**: a period of time over which all inputs are variable

- - Length of long run depends on the production process being considered:

Auto manufacturer may need years to build a new production facility but software firm may need only a month or two to rent and move into a new space

## Total Cost

- Firm’s total cost is the expenditure required to produce a given level of output in the most economical way

## Types of Costs

-: **Short run total cost (TC)**

- **Variable cost (VC)**: costs of inputs that vary with the firm’s output level

- **Fixed cost (FC)**: costs of inputs whose use does not vary with the firm’s output level and cannot be varied

## Types of Costs

- Variable cost function: _Variable Cost = VC(Output)_ - Fixed cost function: _Fixed Cost = FC_ - Total cost function (includes fixed costs): _Total Cost = FC + VC(Output)_
