## Transactions involving time

Transactions involving time: - Agents can move resources from one period to another by saving and borrowing

- **Principal**: amount borrowed when one person lends money to another

- **Interest**: amount of money a borrower is obliged to pay a lender, over the principal (compensation) - **Interest Rate**: amount of interest paid on a loan during a particular period, stated as a percentage of the principal

- We call R the  interest rate stated in decimal

## Present value and the price of a future dollar

- In lending, money is the good and interest is the price of having that good for a given period - If you deposit 100 dollar today and the interest rate is 10% a year, next year you get 110 dollars - How much money you need to deposit today if you want to get M dollars next year? - X=M/1+R

- X is called the **present discounted value (PDV** ) of  next year M dollars
