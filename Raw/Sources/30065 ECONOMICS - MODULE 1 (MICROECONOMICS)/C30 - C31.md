## Asymmetric information

- **asymmetric Information:** one of the 2 parts of a transaction has more information than the other on the exchanged good or service.

- Example: a worker on his ability or effort, the owner of an apartment on the quality of the apartment, a manager on the effort he puts in a project

- If information is not equally shared: inefficiency

- 2 types of asymmetric information:

- **Hidden Characteristics**: Things that one side of a transaction knows about itself that the other side would like to know but does not – e.g. Adverse Selection

- **Hidden Actions**: Actions taken by one side of an economic relationship that the other side cannot observe– e.g. moral hazard

## Moral hazard

- Economic situation:

- **Principal** delegates a project to an **agent**

- The action chosen by the agent influences but does not totally determine the outcome

- Principal and agent have different preferences: - The principal prefers a high effort by the agent, while the agent prefers to work with lowest effort

- Unobservability of actions +difference In preferences give rise to **moral hazard**

- _Moral hazard: agent takes the less costly action that negatively influences the final outcome._ - Inefficiency

## Remedies

- What contract may induce the agent to take the principal preferred action?

- Problem: fixed wage- action not observable→ agent takes the least costly one

- Remedy: wage based on the observable outcome

## Remedies

- Trade-off variable wage

- Gives incentives to the agent to take high effort(+) - Costly for the principal because involves some risk for the agent(-)
