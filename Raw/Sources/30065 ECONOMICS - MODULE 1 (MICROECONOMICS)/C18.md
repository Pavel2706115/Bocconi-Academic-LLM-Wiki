## Cournot quantity competition 

Ø **Cournot model of oligopoly:** firms choose how much to produce (Q) simultaneously, and the price clears the market given the total quantity produced 

ØAssume homogeneous goods ØTo keep things simple we study a duopoly 

## How to solve the game? 

# ØSimultaneous moves game ØInfinite set of actions Ø _Thus:_ 

- 1- find best response functions 

2- see where they cross each other: that is NE 

## Firms’ profits 

Firm 1: PxQ1-TC1 Firm 2: PxQ2-TC2 Where P is the (total) inverse demand: P(Q1+Q2) 

## Step 1: Best responses 

ØTake derivative of profits for each firm èSet MR equal to MC for each firm 

ØMR =MC 1 1 ØMR =MC 2 2 

èfind best response functions! 

## Step 2: Nash Equilibrium 

Solve the system of equations: Q1=f(Q2) Q2=f(Q1) 

èFind NE: (Q1,Q2) 

èTo find P use inverse demand function

## Best response functions with identical firms 

When two firms have the same cost structure, and face the same demand, they have the same best response function and thus they will produce the same amount of output. Q =3000-0.5Q 1 2 Q =3000-0.5Q 2 1 

## Cournot competition vs Bertrand Model 

ØImportant difference from equilibrium in Bertrand market: in Cournot, equilibrium price is always above marginal cost ØDeadweight loss in Cournot! 

## Deadweight Loss from Duopoly versus Monopoly 

ØWill the aggregate surplus be higher in the Bertrand and Cournot equilibrium compared to the monopoly outcome? ØIn Bertrand Model: DWL=0 

- ØIn Cournot Model: DWL smaller than in Monopoly 

ØThe deadweight loss of monopoly is larger because the monopoly price is further above marginal cost than is the oligopoly price
