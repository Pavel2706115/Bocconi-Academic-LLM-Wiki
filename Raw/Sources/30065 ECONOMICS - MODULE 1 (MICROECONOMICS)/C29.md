## Insurance 

ØPeople can protect their wealth (W) against risky events (lotteries) purchasing insurance policies 

ØAn _**insurance policy**_ is a contract that, at a price, reduces the financial loss associated with a risky event: 

ØIt specifies a **BENEFIT** (B), the amount the policyholder receives if the loss occurs, and a **PREMIUM** (P), the price of the insurance. 

ØFull coverage: it covers the whole wealth (B=W) (we look at this case) ØPartial coverage: it covers only in part (we do not study it) 

ØWe assume there are only two possible outcomes of a risky event: a bad outcome (loss) with probability _p_ and a good outcome (no loss) with probability ( _1-p_ )

## Insurance 

ØFull insurance coverage turns a lottery into a riskless lottery. Having paid the premium: 

Øif a loss occurs, the policy holder receives  the benefit, for a net gain of 

_Benefit – Premium_ 

ØIf a loss does not occur the consumer keeps the object: _Value of the object(=Benefit) – Premium_ 

- ØAgents under full insurance end up having _with certainty_ a wealth lower than the initial one, W.

## Exam le p 

ØGiovanni owns a painting which he values at W=900 euros ØThe probability of the painting being stolen is: p=0.5 

ØThis situation can be interpreted as a lottery: ØWith probability 0.5 Giovanni keeps the painting and gets 900 euros ØWith probability 0.5 Giovanni gets robbed and ends up with 0 euros 

ØIf Giovanni decides to fully insure the painting and pays a premium P: 

Ø in case of a robbery he has 900-P  euros ØIn case of no robbery he has 900-P euros 

èthe insurance is turning the lottery into a riskless one

## Company side: size of the remium p 

ØHow much would an insurance company ask in order to insure an agent? 

ØA company decides to offer an insurance policy only if it makes non-negative expected profit (risk neutrality) 

ØThe expected profits of a company are given by: 

_E_ Π = _P_ − _pB_ − 1(− _p_ )0 where _P_ is the premium, B is the benefit and p is the probability of the bad outcome

Ø . If P>pB, the premium is called _actuarially unfair_

## A ents’ side g 

ØWould agents be willing to be insured for a premium P≥pB? 

Ø In order to answer we need to compare expected utilities with and without insurance. 

_EU(no insurance)=pu(0)+(1-p)u(W)_ 

_U(insurance)=pu(W-P)+(1-p)u(W-P)=u(W-P)_ 

ØThen, an agent is willing to buy full coverage if and only if: 

_U(insurance)≥EU(no insurance)_ 

ØLet us first compute the expected value of the lottery with and without insurance and then discuss the point for each type of agent, according to his preferences toward risk.

## Risk-loving and risk-neutral a ents g 

ØIf a risk loving agent is offered an actuarially fair contract, he will never choose full coverage ØEV(fair insurance)=EV(no insurance) èU(fair insurance)<EU(no insurance) 

ØIf a risk neutral agent is offered an actuarially fair contract, he will be indifferent between full coverage and no insurance ØEV(fair insurance)=EV(no insurance) èU(fair insurance)=EU(no insurance) 

ØRisk loving and risk neutral agents would not buy full coverage if the premium is actuarially unfair. 

ØEV(unfair insurance)<EV(no insurance) èU(unfair insurance)<EU(no insurance)

## Risk-averse a ents g 

ØIf a risk averse agent is offered an actuarially fair contract, he will choose full coverage 

ØThe actuarially fair contract gives him his expected wealth for sure: 

ØEV(fair insurance)=EV(no insurance) 

èU(fair insurance)>EU(no insurance) 

ØIf a risk averse agent is offered an actuarially unfair contract, the maximum premium he is willing to pay is such that, once paid, he is left with the certainty equivalent of the lottery: P≤W-CE 

ØIf P=W-CE:  EU(no insurance)=U(unfair insurance) 

## ØSumming up: 

ØAn actuarially fair insurance leaves a risk averse agent with the EV for sure 

ØA less than actuarially fair insurance must leave the agent with **at least** the CE for sure
