## Effects of a specific tax T on sellers 

Ø For any price paid by consumers, firms now receive less than when there is no tax 

Ø Won’t be willing to supply as much as before 

Ø Supply curve with the tax is a distance T above the original supply curve 

Ø New equilibrium price paid by consumers is price at which the demand curve and new supply curve cross 

Ø Amount bought and sold falls 

Ø Price paid by consumers rises; price received by firms falls

## Incidence of a specific Tax 

Ø **Incidence of a tax:** how much of the tax burden is borne by various market participants. ØIt depends on elasticity of demand and supply curves Ø 4 cases: 

ØPerfectly elastic demand: sellers bear the entire burden of the tax ØPerfectly inelastic supply: sellers bear the entire burden of the tax ØPerfectly inelastic demand: buyers bear the entire burden of the tax ØPerfectly elastic supply: buyers bear the entire burden of the tax
