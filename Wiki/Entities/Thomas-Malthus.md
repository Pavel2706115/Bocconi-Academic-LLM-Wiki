# Thomas Malthus

## Overview

Thomas Robert Malthus (1766-1834) was an English economist and demographer. His *Essay on the Principle of Population* (1798) argued that population growth would outstrip food production, leading to famine and poverty.

## Key Contributions

- **Malthusian trap**: population grows geometrically while food production grows arithmetically
- **Positive checks**: famine, disease, war — reduce population
- **Preventive checks**: delayed marriage, abstinence — limit births
- Influenced Darwin's theory of natural selection

## Relevance

- **Economic History**: explains pre-industrial stagnation
- **Public Finance**: early thinking on poverty and welfare policy
