# SEC

## Overview

The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (est. 1934) is the federal agency responsible for regulating securities markets, enforcing securities law, and protecting investors. Created in response to the 1929 stock market crash.

## Key Functions

1. Require public companies to disclose financial information
2. Enforce securities law against fraud and insider trading
3. Oversee securities exchanges, brokers, and investment advisers
4. Delegate accounting standard-setting to FASB

## Relevance

- **Financial Accounting**: oversees GAAP enforcement and financial disclosure
- **Financial Markets**: core regulatory body for US capital markets
- **Financial Regulation**: model for securities regulation globally
