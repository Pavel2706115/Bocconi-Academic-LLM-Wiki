# ECB

## Overview

The European Central Bank (est. 1998, Frankfurt) is the central bank for the Eurozone (20 member states as of 2024). Its primary mandate is price stability (inflation close to but below 2%).

## Structure

- **Governing Council**: 6 Executive Board members + national central bank governors
- **Executive Board**: President, VP, and 4 members — manage day-to-day operations
- **Eurosystem**: ECB + national central banks of Eurozone members

## Key Functions

1. Set monetary policy for the Eurozone
2. Conduct foreign exchange operations
3. Hold and manage official foreign reserves
4. Promote smooth payment system operation
