# John Maynard Keynes

## Overview

John Maynard Keynes (1883-1946) was a British economist whose ideas fundamentally transformed macroeconomic theory and government policy. His *General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money* (1936) argued for active government intervention to stabilize the economy.

## Key Contributions

- **Aggregate demand**: output and employment are determined by total spending
- **Liquidity preference**: demand for money as a function of income and interest rates
- **Fiscal policy**: government spending can offset private sector shortfalls
- **Paradox of thrift**: individual saving during recession worsens the downturn
- **Animal spirits**: investor confidence drives investment and business cycles

## Relevance

- **Macroeconomics**: IS-LM model, fiscal multipliers, goods market equilibrium
- **Economic History**: Keynesian response to the Great Depression; Bretton Woods architect
- **Public Finance**: justification for countercyclical fiscal policy
