# IMF

## Overview

The International Monetary Fund (est. 1944 at the Bretton Woods Conference) promotes international monetary cooperation, exchange rate stability, and orderly financial conditions. It provides financial assistance to countries with balance of payments difficulties.

## Key Functions

1. Surveillance: monitoring global economy and member country policies
2. Lending: providing temporary financial assistance with conditionality
3. Technical assistance: helping countries design policy reforms

## Relevance

- **Economic History**: central institution of the Bretton Woods system
- **Macroeconomics**: international monetary policy coordination
- **Financial Markets**: financial stability and crisis management
