# Adam Smith

## Overview

Adam Smith (1723-1790) was a Scottish philosopher and economist, widely considered the father of modern economics. His seminal work *The Wealth of Nations* (1776) laid the foundations for classical economics and free-market theory.

## Key Contributions

- **Invisible hand**: individuals pursuing self-interest inadvertently promote social welfare
- **Division of labor**: specialization increases productivity
- **Free trade**: comparative advantage benefits all trading nations
- **Theory of Moral Sentiments**: self-interest is tempered by sympathy and social norms

## Relevance to Courses

- **Microeconomics**: supply and demand, competitive markets
- **Economic History**: mercantilist critique, role of institutions in development
- **Public Finance**: the role of government in providing public goods
