# Marketing

## Overview

Marketing (30705) is the academic and practical discipline focused on creating, delivering, and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners, and society at large. This course covers sustainable marketing and trust, competitive environment analysis, quantitative market demand estimation, consumer decision-making models, distribution channels, strategic segmentation/targeting/positioning, product range management, pricing strategies, integrated marketing communication, and trade marketing.

## Core Concepts

- [[Wiki/Concepts/Sustainable-Marketing-and-Trust|Sustainable Marketing and Trust]] — Business orientations, Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), and Corporate Social Responsibility
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Environment-and-Competition|Marketing Environment and Competition]] — Macro & micro environments, Porter's Five Forces, and Strategic Groups
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Demand-Estimation-and-Market-Share|Demand Estimation and Market Share]] — Quantitative demand analysis, market potential calculations, and market share decomposition
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Consumer-Behavior-Models|Consumer Behavior Models]] — Purchasing processes, psychological involvement, Fishbein model, and non-compensatory choices
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Commercial-Distribution-and-Retail|Commercial Distribution and Retail]] — Role of intermediaries, retail distribution formats, and commercial services
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Segmentation-and-Positioning|Marketing Segmentation and Positioning]] — B2C & B2B segmentation criteria, target segment selection, and perceptual positioning mapping
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Product-Range-and-Branding|Product Range and Branding]] — Product mix breadth/depth, branding strategies (house of brands vs. branded house), and BCG matrix
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Pricing-Policies-and-Bundling|Pricing Policies and Bundling]] — Cost/demand/competition-based pricing, break-even settings, price discrimination, and bundling
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Marketing-Communication-and-Advertising|Marketing Communication and Advertising]] — Integrated communication mix, advertising brief structure, media planning, and ROI metrics
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Distribution-Channels-and-Trade-Marketing|Distribution Channels and Trade Marketing]] — Direct/indirect channel designs, push/pull policies, and trade marketing tactics

## Key Takeaways

- Marketing orientation prioritizes long-term trust, customer lifetime value, and sustainable development over transaction-based sales.
- Measuring market share requires breaking it down into penetration and weighted coverage indexes to identify competitive strengths and distributor portfolio quality.
- Strategic marketing is defined by the STP process: segmenting the demand, targeting highly attractive audiences, and positioning the offer uniquely in the consumer's perceptual space.
- Pricing decisions must balance cost bounds (break-even point), demand ceiling (customer indifference price), and competitive positioning differentials.
