# Management

## Overview

Management (30060) introduces managerial decision-making frameworks, combining quantitative methods (probability, decision analysis, break-even analysis) with management accounting (cost classification, financial statement construction, performance measurement).

## Core Concepts

- [[Wiki/Concepts/Decision-Analysis|Decision Analysis]] — Scientific approach to decision-making under uncertainty
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Conditional-Probability|Conditional Probability]] — Bayesian updating and prediction of events
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Break-Even-Analysis|Break-Even Analysis]] — Cost-volume-profit relationships
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Cost-Accounting|Cost Accounting]] — Cost classification, allocation, and management
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Financial-Statements-for-Managers|Financial Statements for Managers]] — Reading and building financial statements

## Key Takeaways

- Good decisions require probabilistic thinking and systematic data collection
- Conditional probabilities help update beliefs as new information arrives (Bayesian reasoning)
- Break-even analysis identifies the point where total revenue equals total cost
- Managers must understand financial statements to evaluate performance and make resource allocation decisions
