# Financial Accounting

## Overview

Financial Accounting (30692 Module 1 + 30693 Module 2) teaches how to prepare, read, and analyze financial statements under GAAP and IFRS. Module 1 covers the four basic statements, the accounting cycle, assets, liabilities, and revenue recognition. Module 2 extends to cash flow statements, financial statement analysis, consolidation, income taxes, and investments. Based on Libby, Libby & Hodge (12th edition).

## Core Concepts

- [[Wiki/Concepts/Balance-Sheet|Balance Sheet]] — Assets, liabilities, and stockholders' equity at a point in time
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Income-Statement|Income Statement]] — Revenues minus expenses over a period
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Cash-Flow-Statement|Cash Flow Statement]] — Operating, investing, and financing cash flows
- [[Wiki/Concepts/GAAP-and-IFRS|GAAP and IFRS]] — Accounting standards frameworks
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Revenue-Recognition|Revenue Recognition]] — When and how to record revenue
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Depreciation-and-Amortization|Depreciation and Amortization]] — Allocating long-lived asset costs over time
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Financial-Statement-Analysis|Financial Statement Analysis]] — Ratios, trends, and comparative analysis
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Consolidated-Financial-Statements|Consolidated Financial Statements]] — Combining parent and subsidiary accounts
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Accounting-Equation|Accounting Equation]] — Assets = Liabilities + Stockholders' Equity

## Key Takeaways

- The four basic financial statements provide complementary views of a company's financial health
- GAAP (US) and IFRS (international) provide the rules for consistent financial reporting
- Cash flow from operations is the most important indicator of a firm's ability to generate cash
- Financial statement analysis uses ratios to evaluate profitability, liquidity, and solvency
