# Economic History

## Overview

Economic History (30067) traces the evolution of economic systems from pre-industrial agrarian societies through industrialization, globalization, crises, and the post-war order. Based on Alfani-Colli with primary source readings. Taught by Sonia Schifano.

## Core Concepts

- [[Wiki/Concepts/Pre-Industrial-Economy|Pre-Industrial Economy]] — Agricultural societies, the Malthusian trap, and the Black Death
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Great-Divergence|Great Divergence]] — Why Europe industrialised first
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Industrial-Revolution|Industrial Revolution]] — Technological change, factory system, and economic transformation
- [[Wiki/Concepts/First-Globalisation|First Globalisation]] — 19th century trade, migration, and capital flows
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Great-Depression|Great Depression]] — The 1929 crisis and its worldwide consequences
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Bretton-Woods-System|Bretton Woods System]] — Post-war international monetary order
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Post-War-Prosperity|Post-War Prosperity]] — The Golden Age of capitalism (1945-1973)
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Decolonization-and-Asian-Tigers|Decolonization and Asian Tigers]] — Divergent development paths after WWII

## Key Takeaways

- Pre-industrial economies were constrained by the Malthusian relationship between population and resources
- The Industrial Revolution was a uniquely European phenomenon driven by institutions, technology, and capital
- Financial crises recur throughout history with remarkably similar patterns
- International monetary systems (gold standard, Bretton Woods, floating rates) shape global economic outcomes
