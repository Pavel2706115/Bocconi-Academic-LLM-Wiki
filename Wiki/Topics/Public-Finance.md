# Public Finance

## Overview

Public Finance (30264) studies the proper role of government in the economy: when to intervene, how to intervene, and what the effects are. Covers market failures, social insurance programs (education, healthcare, pensions), and the theory and practice of taxation. Based on Gruber's Public Finance and Public Policy. Coordinated by Prof. Paola Profeta.

## Core Concepts

- [[Wiki/Concepts/Market-Failures|Market Failures]] — Why free markets sometimes fail to achieve efficiency
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Public-Goods|Public Goods]] — Non-rival, non-excludable goods and free-riding
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Externalities|Externalities]] — Spillover costs and benefits; Pigouvian taxes and subsidies
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Social-Insurance|Social Insurance]] — Government programs to insure against adverse events
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Healthcare-Economics|Healthcare Economics]] — Market failures in health and insurance design
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Pension-Systems|Pension Systems]] — Pay-as-you-go vs funded systems; Social Security design
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Education-Economics|Education Economics]] — Returns, voucher programs, teacher value-added, and class-size effects
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Taxation-Principles|Taxation Principles]] — Tax bases, rates, progressivity, and statutory/economic incidence
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Tax-Inefficiency|Tax Inefficiency]] — Deadweight loss, Harberger triangles, and Ramsey optimal tax rules
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Capital-and-Wealth-Taxation|Capital and Wealth Taxation]] — Savings choices, inflation dynamics, risk-taking, and estate/property taxes
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Consumption-Taxation|Consumption Taxation]] — VAT design, direct/indirect taxation, and tax compliance and evasion
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Income-Redistribution|Income Redistribution]] — Government's role in reducing inequality

## Key Takeaways

- Government intervention is justified by market failures (externalities, public goods, asymmetric info) and redistribution
- The four questions of public finance: When, how, what effects, and why governments intervene the way they do
- All taxes create distortions (deadweight loss); the goal is to minimize inefficiency while raising revenue
- Social insurance programs must balance coverage, incentives, and fiscal sustainability
