# Corporate Finance

## Overview

Corporate Finance (30017) studies how firms make investment and financing decisions. Part A covers investment decisions and instruments (NPV, IRR, bonds, equity). Part B covers risk and return (portfolio theory, CAPM, cost of capital). Part C covers capital structure (Modigliani-Miller, leverage, payout policy).

## Core Concepts

- [[Wiki/Concepts/Net-Present-Value|Net Present Value (NPV)]] — The fundamental investment decision rule
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Internal-Rate-of-Return|Internal Rate of Return (IRR)]] — Alternative investment metric and its pitfalls
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Capital-Budgeting|Capital Budgeting]] — Practical project evaluation with inflation, depreciation, and working capital
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Bond-Valuation|Bond Valuation]] — Fixed income pricing, spot rates, forward rates
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Equity-Valuation|Equity Valuation]] — DDM, Gordon Growth Model, free cash flow valuation
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Portfolio-Theory|Portfolio Theory]] — Diversification, efficient frontier, and risk-return tradeoff
- [[Wiki/Concepts/CAPM|CAPM]] — Capital Asset Pricing Model and systematic risk
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Cost-of-Capital|Cost of Capital]] — WACC and its components
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Capital-Structure|Capital Structure]] — Modigliani-Miller, leverage, and optimal financing

## Key Takeaways

- Invest when NPV > 0 (project returns exceed the opportunity cost of capital)
- Diversification eliminates idiosyncratic risk; only systematic risk is priced
- CAPM links expected return to market beta
- Capital structure decisions affect firm value through taxes, bankruptcy costs, and agency problems
