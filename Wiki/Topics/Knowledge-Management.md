# Knowledge Management

## Overview

Knowledge management in the context of an LLM Wiki is the practice of organizing information into a two-layer system: **Raw sources** and **compiled Wiki notes**.

Raw sources preserve original context — lecture slides, transcripts, articles — exactly as they were created. Wiki notes distill those sources into concise, reusable knowledge entries that are easy to search, link, and maintain.

## Core Concepts

- [[Wiki/Concepts/Source-Wiki-Separation|Source-Wiki Separation]] — The fundamental architectural pattern
- Search-first workflow — Query the catalog before browsing raw material

## Key Takeaways

- Separate capture from compilation
- Every claim must trace back to a source
- Automated tooling keeps the system honest
- AI agents can ingest, query, and maintain the Wiki
