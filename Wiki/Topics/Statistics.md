# Statistics

## Overview

Statistics (30001) covers the mathematical foundations of data analysis, inference, and modelling. The course progresses from descriptive statistics through probability theory, estimation, hypothesis testing, and regression analysis.

## Core Concepts

- [[Wiki/Concepts/Descriptive-Statistics|Descriptive Statistics]] — Summarizing data with tables, charts, and numerical measures
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Probability-Distributions|Probability Distributions]] — Discrete and continuous probability models
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Sampling-and-Central-Limit-Theorem|Sampling and Central Limit Theorem]] — Foundations of statistical inference
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Point-Estimation|Point Estimation]] — Estimating population parameters from sample statistics
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Interval-Estimation|Interval Estimation]] — Confidence intervals for means and proportions
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Hypothesis-Testing|Hypothesis Testing]] — Formal framework for testing statistical claims
- [[Wiki/Concepts/Linear-Regression|Linear Regression]] — Simple and multiple regression modelling

## Key Takeaways

- Statistics provides the inferential bridge from sample data to population-level conclusions
- The Central Limit Theorem underpins virtually all classical inference methods
- Regression analysis links dependent and independent variables for prediction and explanation
- Understanding p-values, confidence intervals, and test statistics is essential for evidence-based decision making
