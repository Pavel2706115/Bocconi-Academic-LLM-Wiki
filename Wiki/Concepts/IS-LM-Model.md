# IS-LM Model

## Definition

The IS-LM model describes the simultaneous equilibrium in the goods market (IS curve) and the money/financial market (LM curve), determining output (Y) and the interest rate (i).

## IS Curve (Investment-Saving)

- Combinations of (Y, i) where the goods market is in equilibrium
- Downward sloping: higher i → lower investment → lower Y
- Shifts with changes in G, T, or autonomous consumption/investment

## LM Curve (Liquidity-Money)

- Combinations of (Y, i) where money demand = money supply
- Under modern central banking: LM is effectively horizontal at the policy rate
- The central bank sets i; money supply adjusts endogenously

## Policy Analysis

| Policy | IS shift | Effect on Y | Effect on i |
|--------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Fiscal expansion (↑G or ↓T) | Right | ↑ | ↑ |
| Monetary expansion (↓i) | — (LM shifts down) | ↑ | ↓ |
