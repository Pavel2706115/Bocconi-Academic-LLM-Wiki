# Cost of Capital

## Definition

The cost of capital is the minimum rate of return a firm must earn on its investments to satisfy its investors. It represents the opportunity cost of using the firm's capital.

## Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)

WACC = (E/V) × rₑ + (D/V) × r_d × (1 − T)

Where:
- E/V = equity weight, D/V = debt weight
- rₑ = cost of equity (from CAPM)
- r_d = cost of debt (yield on existing debt)
- T = corporate tax rate

## Components

- **Cost of equity**: estimated via CAPM or DDM
- **Cost of debt**: observable from bond yields; tax-deductible
- **Weights**: based on market values, not book values
