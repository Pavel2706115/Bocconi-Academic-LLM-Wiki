# Financial Statement Analysis

## Definition

Financial statement analysis uses quantitative techniques — primarily ratio analysis — to evaluate a company's financial performance, position, and cash-generating ability.

## Key Ratio Categories

### Profitability
- **ROE**: Net Income / Stockholders' Equity
- **ROA**: Net Income / Total Assets
- **Net profit margin**: Net Income / Revenue

### Liquidity
- **Current ratio**: Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- **Quick ratio**: (Current Assets − Inventory) / Current Liabilities

### Solvency
- **Debt-to-equity**: Total Debt / Stockholders' Equity
- **Interest coverage**: EBIT / Interest Expense

### Efficiency
- **Asset turnover**: Revenue / Total Assets
- **Inventory turnover**: COGS / Average Inventory
- **Receivables turnover**: Revenue / Average AR

### Market
- **P/E ratio**: Price per Share / EPS
- **Market-to-book**: Market Cap / Book Value of Equity

## DuPont Analysis

ROE = Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier
