# Fiscal Policy

## Definition

Fiscal policy refers to government decisions about spending (G) and taxation (T) to influence aggregate demand, output, and employment.

## Mechanisms

- **Expansionary**: ↑G or ↓T → IS shifts right → ↑Y
- **Contractionary**: ↓G or ↑T → IS shifts left → ↓Y
- **Automatic stabilizers**: tax revenues and transfers that automatically dampen business cycle fluctuations

## Fiscal Multipliers

- Government spending multiplier: ΔY/ΔG = 1/(1−c₁)
- Tax multiplier: ΔY/ΔT = −c₁/(1−c₁)
- Balanced budget multiplier = 1

## Considerations

- **Crowding out**: higher G may raise interest rates, reducing private investment
- **Government debt**: persistent deficits increase debt-to-GDP ratio
- **Ricardian equivalence**: rational consumers may save tax cuts, anticipating future tax increases
