# Phillips Curve

## Definition

The Phillips Curve describes the empirical and theoretical relationship between inflation and unemployment. In the short run, lower unemployment is associated with higher inflation.

## Versions

- **Original Phillips Curve**: inverse relationship between wage inflation and unemployment
- **Expectations-Augmented**: πₜ = πₜᵉ − α(uₜ − uₙ) — inflation depends on expected inflation and the unemployment gap
- **NAIRU**: Non-Accelerating Inflation Rate of Unemployment — the "natural" rate

## Key Implications

- Short run: central bank can reduce unemployment below uₙ, but only at the cost of higher inflation
- Medium run: expectations adjust → economy returns to uₙ with higher inflation baked in
- No long-run tradeoff: the long-run Phillips Curve is vertical at uₙ
