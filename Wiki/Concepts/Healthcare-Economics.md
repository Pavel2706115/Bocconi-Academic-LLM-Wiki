# Healthcare Economics

## Definition

Healthcare markets exhibit multiple market failures — asymmetric information, externalities, and incomplete markets — making government intervention particularly important.

## Market Failures in Healthcare

- **Asymmetric information**: patients can't evaluate medical quality; insurers can't observe patient health
- **Adverse selection in insurance**: sick people more likely to buy → premiums rise → healthy exit
- **Moral hazard**: insured patients overconsume healthcare
- **Externalities**: vaccination benefits society beyond the individual

## System Designs

- **Single-payer** (e.g., UK NHS, Canada): government provides/finances care
- **Social insurance** (e.g., Germany, France): mandatory insurance with multiple payers
- **Mixed** (e.g., US): combination of private and public (Medicare, Medicaid, ACA)
