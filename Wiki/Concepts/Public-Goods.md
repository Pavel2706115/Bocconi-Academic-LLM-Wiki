# Public Goods

## Definition

Public goods are goods that are non-rival (one person's consumption doesn't reduce availability for others) and non-excludable (impossible to prevent non-payers from consuming).

## Classification

| | Excludable | Non-Excludable |
|--|-----------|----------------|
| **Rival** | Private good (food) | Common resource (fish in ocean) |
| **Non-Rival** | Club good (cable TV) | Public good (national defense) |

## Free Rider Problem

Since no one can be excluded, individuals have incentive to consume without paying → private markets underprovide public goods.

## Solutions

- Government provision (financed by taxes)
- Voluntary contributions (often insufficient)
- Pigouvian subsidies for positive externalities
