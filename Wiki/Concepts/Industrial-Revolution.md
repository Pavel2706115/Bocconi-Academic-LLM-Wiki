# Industrial Revolution

## Definition

The Industrial Revolution (c. 1760-1840 in Britain, spreading to Europe and beyond) was a period of fundamental economic transformation — from agrarian handicraft to mechanised factory production.

## Key Innovations

- **Textile machinery**: spinning jenny, water frame, power loom
- **Steam engine**: James Watt's improvements enabled factories and railways
- **Iron and steel**: coke-smelting replaced charcoal; Bessemer process

## Social Consequences

- Urbanisation and population growth
- Factory labour system and child labour
- Rise of the middle class and working class
- Environmental degradation

## Spread Beyond Britain

- Belgium, France, Germany industrialised in the 19th century
- Japan (Meiji Restoration, 1868) and Russia industrialised later
- Latin America and most of Asia/Africa remained predominantly agricultural
