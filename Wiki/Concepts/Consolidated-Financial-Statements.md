# Consolidated Financial Statements

## Definition

Consolidated financial statements combine the financial statements of a parent company and its subsidiaries as if they were a single economic entity. Required when a parent controls (>50% voting rights) a subsidiary.

## Key Procedures

- Eliminate intercompany transactions (sales, loans, dividends)
- Eliminate intercompany balances (receivables/payables between entities)
- Recognize non-controlling interest (minority shareholders' share)
- Adjust for goodwill arising from acquisition at premium
