# Supply and Demand

## Definition

Supply and demand are the fundamental market forces that determine the price and quantity of goods traded. The demand curve shows how much consumers want to buy at each price; the supply curve shows how much producers want to sell.

## Demand

- **Law of demand**: quantity demanded decreases as price increases (downward sloping)
- **Determinants**: price of good, prices of related goods (substitutes/complements), income (normal vs inferior goods), tastes, demographics
- **Shift vs movement**: price changes cause movement along the curve; other factors shift the curve

## Supply

- **Law of supply**: quantity supplied increases as price increases (upward sloping)
- **Determinants**: price, technology, input prices, taxes/regulation, number of firms
