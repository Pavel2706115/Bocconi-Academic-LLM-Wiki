# GAAP and IFRS

## Definition

GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles) and IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards) are the two main accounting frameworks governing how financial statements are prepared.

## GAAP (US)

- Set by the **FASB** (Financial Accounting Standards Board)
- Overseen by the **SEC**
- Rules-based: detailed, specific rules for each scenario

## IFRS (International)

- Set by the **IASB** (International Accounting Standards Board)
- Adopted by 147+ jurisdictions (EU, UK, Canada, Australia, etc.)
- Principles-based: broader guidelines allowing professional judgment

## Key Differences

- Revenue recognition timing
- Inventory methods (LIFO allowed under GAAP, prohibited under IFRS)
- Development costs (capitalized under IFRS, expensed under GAAP)
