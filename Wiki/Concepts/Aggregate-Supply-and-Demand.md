# Aggregate Supply and Demand

## Definition

The goods market equilibrium determines aggregate output (Y) given demand components. In the short run, the Keynesian multiplier amplifies changes in autonomous spending.

## Goods Market Equilibrium

Y = C + I + G + NX

- **Consumption**: C = c₀ + c₁(Y − T), where c₁ = MPC
- **Investment**: autonomous or interest-sensitive
- **Government spending**: fiscal policy lever

## The Multiplier

Multiplier = 1 / (1 − c₁)

A €1 increase in autonomous spending increases equilibrium output by more than €1 due to the circular flow of income.
