# Great Depression

## Definition

The Great Depression (1929-1939) was the most severe global economic downturn of the 20th century, triggered by the 1929 Wall Street crash and amplified by bank failures, deflation, and protectionism.

## Causes

- Speculative bubble in stocks
- Tight monetary policy (gold standard constraint)
- Banking panics and credit contraction
- Smoot-Hawley tariffs → collapse of international trade
- Debt deflation spiral (Irving Fisher)

## Consequences

- US GDP fell ~30%; unemployment reached 25%
- International trade collapsed by ~65%
- Rise of authoritarian regimes in Europe
- Birth of modern macroeconomics (Keynes' General Theory, 1936)
