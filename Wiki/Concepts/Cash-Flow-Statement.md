# Cash Flow Statement

## Definition

The statement of cash flows reports the inflows and outflows of cash during an accounting period, classified into operating, investing, and financing activities.

## Three Sections

### Operating Activities
Cash flows from day-to-day business operations. Two methods:
- **Indirect method**: starts with net income, adjusts for non-cash items and working capital changes
- **Direct method**: reports gross cash receipts and payments

### Investing Activities
Cash flows from buying/selling long-term assets and investments.

### Financing Activities
Cash flows from transactions with creditors and owners (issuing debt/equity, repaying loans, paying dividends).

## Key Ratios

- **Quality of income ratio**: Cash from operations / Net income (higher = better quality)
- **Capital acquisitions ratio**: Cash from operations / Capital expenditures
- **Free cash flow**: Cash from operations − Capital expenditures − Dividends
