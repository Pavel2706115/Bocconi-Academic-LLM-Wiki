# Capital Structure

## Definition

Capital structure is the mix of debt and equity financing a firm uses to fund its operations and investments. The central question: does the choice between debt and equity affect firm value?

## Modigliani-Miller Propositions

### MM I (No Taxes)
In a perfect capital market (no taxes, no bankruptcy costs, no asymmetric info), firm value is independent of capital structure.

### MM I (With Taxes)
The value of a levered firm = value of unlevered firm + PV of tax shield. Debt creates value through tax deductibility of interest.

### MM II
The cost of equity increases linearly with leverage (debt-to-equity ratio), offsetting the benefit of cheaper debt.

## Trade-Off Theory

Optimal capital structure balances:
- **Tax shield of debt** (benefit)
- **Costs of financial distress** and bankruptcy (cost)
- **Agency costs** (conflicts between managers, shareholders, creditors)
