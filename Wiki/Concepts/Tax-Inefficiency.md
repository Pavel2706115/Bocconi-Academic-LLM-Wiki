# Tax Inefficiency

## Definition

All taxes (except lump-sum taxes) create deadweight loss — a reduction in total welfare beyond the revenue collected. This excess burden arises because taxes distort economic decisions.

## Harberger Triangle

The deadweight loss of a tax is approximately proportional to the square of the tax rate:
DWL ≈ ½ × t² × ε × P × Q

Where ε is the elasticity of demand/supply and t is the tax rate.

## Implications

- **Small taxes create small distortions** (quadratic relationship)
- **Broad-based taxes are less distortionary** than narrow ones
- **Taxing inelastic goods** creates less DWL
- **Ramsey rule**: optimal taxes are inversely proportional to elasticity
