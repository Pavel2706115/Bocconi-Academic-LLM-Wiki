# Central Banking and Monetary Policy

## Definition

Central banks are government institutions responsible for managing a nation's money supply and interest rates. Monetary policy refers to the actions taken by central banks to achieve macroeconomic objectives (price stability, full employment, financial stability).

## Key Central Banks

- **Federal Reserve (Fed)**: United States
- **European Central Bank (ECB)**: Eurozone
- **Bank of England (BoE)**: United Kingdom

## Policy Tools

- **Open market operations**: buying/selling government bonds to control money supply
- **Policy interest rate**: the rate at which banks borrow from the central bank (e.g., Fed Funds rate, ECB main refinancing rate)
- **Reserve requirements**: minimum reserves banks must hold
- **Quantitative easing (QE)**: large-scale asset purchases in unconventional times

## Transmission Mechanism

Policy rate change → market interest rates → borrowing costs → investment & consumption → output & inflation
