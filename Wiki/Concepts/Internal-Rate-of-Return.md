# Internal Rate of Return

## Definition

The Internal Rate of Return (IRR) is the discount rate that makes a project's NPV equal to zero. It represents the project's expected rate of return.

## Decision Rule

Invest if IRR > opportunity cost of capital (r)

## Pitfalls of IRR

1. **Lending vs Borrowing**: fails when NPV increases with the discount rate
2. **Multiple IRRs**: cash flows with multiple sign changes can yield multiple IRRs
3. **Mutually exclusive projects**: IRR ignores project scale — a smaller project may have higher IRR but lower NPV
4. **Term structure**: assumes a flat discount rate across all periods

## When IRR Works Well

- For single, independent projects with conventional cash flows (initial outflow followed by inflows)
- As a supplementary metric alongside NPV
