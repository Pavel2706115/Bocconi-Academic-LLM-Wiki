# Externalities

## Definition

In the public finance context, externalities are costs or benefits imposed on third parties. They represent a divergence between private and social costs/benefits, leading to inefficient market outcomes.

## Corrective Policies

- **Pigouvian taxes**: tax = marginal external cost → internalize the externality
- **Subsidies**: for positive externalities (education, R&D, vaccination)
- **Cap-and-trade**: quantity-based regulation with tradable permits
- **Command-and-control regulation**: direct standards and mandates

## Coase Theorem

If property rights are well-defined and transaction costs are zero, private bargaining achieves the efficient outcome regardless of who holds the rights.
