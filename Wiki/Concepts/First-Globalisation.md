# First Globalisation

## Definition

The First Globalisation (c. 1870-1914) was a period of unprecedented integration of global markets through trade, migration, and capital flows, facilitated by steamships, railways, the telegraph, and the gold standard.

## Features

- **Trade**: sharp fall in transport costs → commodity price convergence
- **Migration**: ~60 million Europeans migrated to the Americas, Oceania, and Africa
- **Capital flows**: British capital financed railways and infrastructure worldwide
- **Gold standard**: fixed exchange rates facilitated international trade

## End

World War I (1914) disrupted trade, migration, and capital flows, ending this era of globalisation.
