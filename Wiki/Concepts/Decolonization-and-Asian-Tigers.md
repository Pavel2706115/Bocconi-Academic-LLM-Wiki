# Decolonization and Asian Tigers

## Definition

After WWII, decolonization reshaped the global economy as former colonies gained independence. While many developing nations struggled, the "Asian Tigers" (South Korea, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore) achieved rapid industrialisation through export-led growth strategies.

## Asian Tigers Model

- State-directed industrial policy
- Heavy investment in education and human capital
- Export-oriented manufacturing
- High savings rates and investment

## Contrasting Paths

- **Import substitution** (Latin America, India): protectionist strategy that often led to inefficiency
- **Export-led growth** (East Asia): open trade policy that drove rapid catching up
