# Oligopoly and Game Theory

## Definition

An oligopoly is a market with a few large firms whose decisions are interdependent. Game theory provides the analytical framework for strategic interaction.

## Game Theory Foundations

- **Players**: decision makers (firms)
- **Strategies**: possible actions for each player
- **Payoffs**: outcomes (profits) for each strategy combination
- **Nash Equilibrium**: no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing strategy

## Oligopoly Models

- **Cournot**: firms compete on quantity simultaneously
- **Bertrand**: firms compete on price simultaneously → drives price to MC with identical goods
- **Stackelberg**: sequential quantity competition (leader-follower)
- **Collusion/Cartels**: firms cooperate to act as a monopolist (often unstable)

## Prisoner's Dilemma

Illustrates why firms may fail to cooperate even when cooperation maximizes joint payoffs.
