# Financial Crises

## Definition

A financial crisis is a sharp disruption in financial markets characterised by plunging asset prices, bank failures, and credit freezes. Crises often spill over into the real economy, causing recessions and unemployment.

## Common Pattern (Mishkin Framework)

1. **Credit boom**: excessive lending and risk-taking
2. **Asset bubble**: unsustainable rise in asset prices
3. **Trigger event**: shock that reverses expectations
4. **Banking panic**: depositors withdraw, banks fail
5. **Debt deflation**: falling prices increase real debt burden
6. **Policy response**: central bank as lender of last resort, fiscal stimulus

## Historical Examples

- **1929 Great Depression**: stock market crash, bank failures, deflationary spiral
- **2007-2009 Global Financial Crisis**: subprime mortgages, securitization, Lehman Brothers collapse
- **European Sovereign Debt Crisis**: fiscal imbalances in Greece, Ireland, Portugal, Spain
