# Accounting Equation

## Definition

The accounting equation is the foundation of double-entry bookkeeping:

**Assets = Liabilities + Stockholders' Equity**

Every transaction must maintain this equality. If assets increase, either liabilities or equity must also increase (or another asset must decrease).

## Transaction Analysis

Each transaction affects at least two accounts:
- Borrow cash: Cash ↑ (asset), Notes Payable ↑ (liability)
- Sell goods on credit: Accounts Receivable ↑ (asset), Revenue ↑ (equity via net income)
- Pay expenses: Cash ↓ (asset), Retained Earnings ↓ (equity via expense)
