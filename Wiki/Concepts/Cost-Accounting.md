# Cost Accounting

## Definition

Cost accounting classifies, records, and allocates costs to understand how resources are consumed in producing goods and services. It supports internal decision-making by managers.

## Cost Classifications

*   **Fixed vs Variable**: fixed costs don't change with output; variable costs change proportionally
*   **Direct vs Indirect**: direct costs are traceable to a product; indirect costs (overhead) are shared
*   **Product vs Period**: product costs attach to inventory; period costs are expensed immediately

## Key Methods

*   **Job costing**: costs assigned to specific jobs or batches
*   **Process costing**: costs averaged across identical units
*   **Activity-based costing (ABC)**: allocates overhead using activity drivers

---

## Connection to Marketing

In marketing, cost classifications directly shape **pricing strategies** and profit-maximizing decisions:
*   **Variable Costs**: Establish the absolute *pricing floor*. Selling below unit variable cost generates a negative contribution margin, causing direct financial losses.
*   **Direct and Allocated Indirect Costs**: Used to calculate target markups. Marketers must understand how indirect overheads are allocated (e.g. through ABC) to prevent over-pricing high-volume products or under-pricing complex specialty products.
*   **Period Costs**: Direct marketing expenses (such as national advertising campaigns, trade promotions, and sales force salaries) are period costs. Understanding their magnitude is essential for calculating the break-even sales volume required to justify a marketing campaign.
