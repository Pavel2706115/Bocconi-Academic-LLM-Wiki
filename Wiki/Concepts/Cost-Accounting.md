# Cost Accounting

## Definition

Cost accounting classifies, records, and allocates costs to understand how resources are consumed in producing goods and services. It supports internal decision-making by managers.

## Cost Classifications

- **Fixed vs Variable**: fixed costs don't change with output; variable costs change proportionally
- **Direct vs Indirect**: direct costs are traceable to a product; indirect costs (overhead) are shared
- **Product vs Period**: product costs attach to inventory; period costs are expensed immediately

## Key Methods

- **Job costing**: costs assigned to specific jobs or batches
- **Process costing**: costs averaged across identical units
- **Activity-based costing (ABC)**: allocates overhead using activity drivers
