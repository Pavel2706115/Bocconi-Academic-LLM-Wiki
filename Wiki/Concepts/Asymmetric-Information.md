# Asymmetric Information

## Definition

Asymmetric information arises when one party to a transaction has more or better information than the other. It creates two fundamental problems in financial markets and beyond.

## Two Types

### Adverse Selection (Before the Transaction)
- The uninformed party cannot distinguish good from bad types
- Bad types are more likely to self-select into the transaction
- Example: risky borrowers are most eager to take out loans
- Akerlof's "market for lemons" analogy

### Moral Hazard (After the Transaction)
- Once the transaction occurs, the informed party may change behaviour
- Example: a borrower takes on riskier projects after receiving a loan
- Example: insured person takes less care (fire insurance → arson)

## Mitigation Tools

| Tool | Addresses |
|------|-----------|
| Screening (by uninformed party) | Adverse selection |
| Signalling (by informed party) | Adverse selection |
| Monitoring | Moral hazard |
| Collateral | Both |
| Restrictive covenants | Moral hazard |
| Incentive alignment | Moral hazard |
