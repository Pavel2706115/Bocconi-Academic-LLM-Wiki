# Bretton Woods System

## Definition

The Bretton Woods System (1944-1971) was the international monetary order established after WWII. Currencies were pegged to the US dollar, which was convertible to gold at $35/oz. The system created the IMF and World Bank.

## Key Features

- **Fixed but adjustable exchange rates**: currencies pegged to USD with ±1% bands
- **Dollar-gold link**: USD convertible to gold; other currencies convertible to USD
- **Capital controls**: limited capital mobility to prevent speculative attacks
- **IMF**: provided short-term loans to countries with balance of payments problems

## Collapse (1971)

- US ran persistent balance of payments deficits
- Foreign central banks accumulated more dollars than US gold reserves could support
- Nixon "closed the gold window" → floating exchange rates
