# CAPM

## Definition

The Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) establishes a linear relationship between an asset's expected return and its systematic risk (beta). It is the most widely used model for estimating the cost of equity.

## Formula

E(Rᵢ) = Rƒ + βᵢ × (E(Rₘ) − Rƒ)

Where:
- Rƒ = risk-free rate
- βᵢ = asset's sensitivity to market returns
- E(Rₘ) − Rƒ = market risk premium

## Key Implications

- Only systematic risk (β) is priced — idiosyncratic risk is diversifiable
- Higher β → higher expected return
- The Security Market Line (SML) plots expected return vs beta
- Assets above the SML are undervalued; below are overvalued

## Estimating Beta

- Regression of asset returns on market returns: Rᵢ = α + β × Rₘ + ε
- Industry betas as benchmarks
