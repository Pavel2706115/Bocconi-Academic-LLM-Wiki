# Monetary Policy (Macro)

## Definition

From the macro perspective, monetary policy is the central bank's control over money supply and interest rates to stabilize output and inflation. The LM curve (or policy rate rule) captures this.

## Money Demand

M^d = $Y × L(i): money demand increases with income and decreases with the interest rate.

## Policy Implementation

- Central bank sets the policy interest rate
- Money supply adjusts endogenously to meet demand at that rate
- In the IS-LM framework: lowering i shifts the LM curve down → output rises

## Taylor Rule (Modern Approach)

i = r* + π + a(π − π*) + b(Y − Yₙ)

The central bank raises rates when inflation exceeds target or output exceeds potential.
