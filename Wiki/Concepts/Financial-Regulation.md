# Financial Regulation

## Definition

Financial regulation encompasses the laws, rules, and supervisory practices that govern financial institutions and markets. The aim is to reduce systemic risk, protect depositors and investors, and ensure the stability of the financial system.

## Main Objectives

1. **Reduce asymmetric information problems** (disclosure requirements, auditing)
2. **Ensure financial stability** (capital requirements, stress testing)

## Key Regulatory Tools

- Restrictions on entry (licensing)
- Disclosure and transparency requirements
- Capital adequacy requirements (Basel Accords)
- Deposit insurance (FDIC in US, national schemes in EU)
- Restrictions on assets and activities
- Limits on competition and interest rates
