# Externalities and Market Failures

## Definition

Externalities are costs or benefits of a transaction that affect third parties not involved in the exchange. When externalities exist, the market equilibrium is not socially efficient.

## Types

- **Negative externality**: social cost > private cost (e.g., pollution) → overproduction
- **Positive externality**: social benefit > private benefit (e.g., education, vaccination) → underproduction

## Solutions

- **Pigouvian tax/subsidy**: tax negative externalities at the marginal damage rate; subsidize positive ones
- **Coase theorem**: if property rights are well-defined and transaction costs are low, private bargaining achieves efficiency
- **Cap-and-trade**: set a quantity limit and let firms trade emission permits
- **Regulation**: direct quantity controls (standards, mandates)
