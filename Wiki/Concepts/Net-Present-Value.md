# Net Present Value

## Definition

Net Present Value (NPV) is the difference between the present value of a project's future cash flows and its initial investment cost. It is the fundamental rule for investment decisions in corporate finance.

## Formula

NPV = Σ CFₜ/(1+r)^t − Investment Cost

Where r is the discount rate reflecting the project's risk (opportunity cost of capital).

## Decision Rule

- **Invest if NPV > 0**: the project creates value
- **Reject if NPV < 0**: the project destroys value
- Among mutually exclusive projects, choose the one with the highest NPV

## Key Principles

- A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow (time value of money)
- The discount rate should reflect the risk of the cash flows, not the firm's average cost of capital
- NPV is additive: the NPV of combined projects equals the sum of individual NPVs
