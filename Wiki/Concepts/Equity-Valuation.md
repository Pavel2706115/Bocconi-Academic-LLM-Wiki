# Equity Valuation

## Definition

Equity valuation determines the intrinsic value of a company's stock based on expected future cash flows to shareholders.

## Dividend Discount Model (DDM)

P₀ = Σ DIVₜ / (1+r)^t

### Gordon Growth Model (Constant Growth)

P₀ = DIV₁ / (r − g)

Where g = Plowback Ratio × ROE

## Present Value of Growth Opportunities (PVGO)

P₀ = EPS/r + PVGO

- **Growth stocks**: high PVGO component
- **Income stocks**: stable dividends, low PVGO

## Free Cash Flow Valuation

- Values the entire enterprise using Free Cash Flow (FCF)
- PV = Σ FCFₜ/(1+r)^t + Horizon Value/(1+r)^H
- Horizon value often estimated using constant growth model

## Graham's Value Valuation Criteria

As detailed in [[Wiki/Concepts/Value-Investing|Value Investing]], Benjamin Graham developed alternative quantitative criteria that bypass traditional cash flow discounting:

### 1. Net Current Asset Value (NCAV) Model
Focuses strictly on highly liquid assets to determine liquidation value:
$$\text{NCAV} = \text{Current Assets} - \text{Total Liabilities} - \text{Preferred Stock}$$
Under this method, the stock is undervalued if the market price ($P$) satisfies:
$$P < \frac{2}{3} \times \text{NCAV}$$

### 2. Graham's Growth Valuation Formula
A simplified method to determine the intrinsic value ($V$) of growth stocks based on earnings and long-term expansion:
$$V = \text{EPS} \times (8.5 + 2g)$$
Where:
*   $\text{EPS}$ = Expected trailing 12-month Earnings Per Share
*   $8.5$ = Appropriate base P/E for a no-growth company
*   $g$ = Expected long-term annual growth rate (expressed as a percentage)
