# Consumer Theory

## Definition

Consumer theory models how rational individuals allocate their limited income across goods to maximize utility (satisfaction).

## Key Components

- **Preferences**: represented by indifference curves (convex, downward sloping)
- **Budget constraint**: P₁X₁ + P₂X₂ ≤ Income
- **Optimal choice**: tangency between indifference curve and budget line (MRS = price ratio)

## Effects of Price Changes

- **Substitution effect**: switch to relatively cheaper good (always negative for price increases)
- **Income effect**: change in purchasing power (direction depends on normal vs inferior good)
- **Giffen good**: inferior good where income effect dominates — demand rises as price rises (rare)

## Consumer Surplus

Area between the demand curve and market price — measures welfare gain from trade.
