# Commercial Distribution and Retail

## Definition

**Commercial Distribution** is the structural sector and set of economic processes that manage the physical and informational flow of goods from manufacturers to final consumers. Commercial intermediaries (wholesalers, retailers, distributors) create value by bridging gaps in time, place, and possession, converting bulk production into consumer-friendly retail assortments.

---

## 1. Classification Criteria for Points of Sale

Commercial intermediaries categorize physical points of sale along four core dimensions:

1.  **Product Assortment**: The variety and range of items stocked.
2.  **Customer Base**: Target audience (B2B vs. B2C).
3.  **Sales Methods**: Self-service vs. assisted sales.
4.  **Modernity of the Formula**: Traditional vs. modern structured retail formats.

---

## 2. Retail Distribution Formats

The commercial sector is characterized by distinct, standardized distribution formats:

| Format | Average Size ($m^2$) | Assortment Characteristics | Sales Method / Pricing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Superette / Minimarket** | $100 - 400$ | Narrow range of high-turnover daily food items. | Assisted or self-service; higher convenience pricing. |
| **Supermarket** | $400 - 2,500$ | Broad food assortment plus basic household items. | Self-service; competitive pricing. |
| **Superstore** | $2,500 - 4,500$ | Very broad food range and expanded non-food goods. | Self-service; aggressive promotional pricing. |
| **Hypermarket** | $> 4,500$ | "Everything under one roof": massive food and extensive non-food range. | Self-service; high economies of scale. |
| **Discount Store** | $300 - 1,000$ | Limited range, dominated by unbranded private labels. | Self-service; EDLP (Everyday Low Price). |
| **Convenience Store** | $< 100$ | Extended opening hours; emergency daily items. | High premium convenience pricing. |
| **Cash and Carry** | Varies | Bulk wholesale food and non-food for B2B. | Self-service for business cardholders. |
| **Department Store** | Large | Multiple product categories managed as distinct departments. | Assisted sales; high-end and premium pricing. |
| **Large Specialised Retail Outlets** | Massive | High depth in a single product category ("Category Killers", e.g., electronics, DIY). | Assisted and self-service; competitive pricing. |
| **Factory Outlet Center (FOC)** | Large complex | Direct manufacturer-to-consumer clearance points. | Discontinued or excess stock at high discounts. |

## 3. The Commercial Intermediary's Offer: Commercial Services

Commercial intermediaries do not just sell physical goods; they sell **commercial services** that reduce search and transaction costs for consumers.

### A. Core Services (Logistics & Assortment)
*   **Breaking Bulk**: Converting industrial production runs into small, individual packages.
*   **Assortment Building**: Grouping products from different manufacturers into a single point of sale.
*   **Transport and Storage**: Moving and preserving inventory so it is available at the time of purchase.

### B. Extension of Core Services (Value-Added Services)
Modern retailers compete by extending their service offering:
*   *Pre-sale*: Financial credit options, expert assisted consultation, and convenient parking.
*   *In-store*: Interactive maps, loyalty program kiosks, and recreational shopping designs.
*   *Post-sale*: Delivery, installation, return policies, and maintenance services.
