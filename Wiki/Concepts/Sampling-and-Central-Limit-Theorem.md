# Sampling and Central Limit Theorem

## Definition

The **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)** states that regardless of the population distribution, the sampling distribution of the sample mean approaches a normal distribution as the sample size n becomes large (typically n ≥ 30).

## Key Properties

- **Sampling distribution of x̄**: the distribution of all possible sample means from samples of size n
- **Mean of sampling distribution**: E(x̄) = μ (population mean)
- **Standard error**: SE = σ / √n — decreases as sample size grows
- **Shape**: approximately normal for large n, even if the population is not normal

## Significance

- The CLT is the theoretical foundation for virtually all classical inference (confidence intervals, hypothesis tests)
- It justifies using z-scores and t-scores to make probability statements about sample means
- It explains why larger samples produce more precise estimates
