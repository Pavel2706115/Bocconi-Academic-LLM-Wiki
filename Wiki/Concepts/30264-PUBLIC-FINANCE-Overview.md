# 30264 PUBLIC FINANCE Overview

## Definition
Public Finance (course code **30264**) studies the role of the government in the economy. It addresses when the government should intervene (efficiency and equity rationales), how it intervenes, and what effect those interventions have on welfare and behavior.

---

## Measuring the Size of Government

To assess the scale of public sector involvement, economists use two primary expenditure-based metrics:

1. **Government Expenditures as a % of GDP**:
   $$ \text{Government Expenditures as a \% of GDP} = \frac{\text{Total Public Spending}}{\text{Total GDP}} \times 100 $$

2. **Government Expenditures Per Capita**:
   $$ \text{Government Expenditures Per Capita} = \frac{\text{Total Public Spending}}{\text{Total Population}} $$

## Core Course Modules & Concepts

The curriculum is structured into two core halves: government expenditures/social policy and taxation/fiscal reform.

### Part 1: Government Expenditures and Social Policy
* **Market Failures and Interventions**:
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Externalities|Externalities]]: Pigouvian subsidies/taxes and Coasian bargaining.
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Public-Goods|Public Goods]]: Direct provision and crowding-out models.
* **Social Insurance & Healthcare**:
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Social-Insurance|Social Insurance]]: Risk aversion, moral hazard, and asymmetric information.
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Healthcare-Economics|Healthcare Economics]]: Adverse selection and healthcare single-payer vs. private models.
* **Retirement & Pensions**:
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Pension-Systems|Pension Systems]]: PAYGO equilibrium and fully funded actuarial returns.
* **Human Capital**:
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Education-Economics|Education Economics]]: Returns to education, voucher programs, and teacher quality.

### Part 2: Taxation and Revenue Principles
* **Tax Foundation**:
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Taxation-Principles|Taxation Principles]]: Statutory vs. economic incidence, progressive deductions, and credits.
* **Tax Distortions**:
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Tax-Inefficiency|Tax Inefficiency]]: Deadweight loss triangles and Ramsey optimal tax rules.
* **Capital and Savings Distortions**:
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Capital-and-Wealth-Taxation|Capital and Wealth Taxation]]: Intertemporal savings effects, inflation dynamics, risk-taking, and estate taxes.
* **Consumption Base and Reforms**:
  * [[Wiki/Concepts/Consumption-Taxation|Consumption Taxation]]: Value Added Tax (VAT), IVA in Italy, and Allingham-Sandmo compliance theory.
