# Revenue Recognition

## Definition

Revenue recognition determines when and how revenue is recorded in the financial statements. Under accrual accounting, revenue is recognized when earned (goods delivered or services performed), not necessarily when cash is received.

## Five-Step Model (ASC 606 / IFRS 15)

1. Identify the contract with the customer
2. Identify the performance obligations
3. Determine the transaction price
4. Allocate the price to performance obligations
5. Recognize revenue when each obligation is satisfied

## Key Principles

- Revenue is earned when the performance obligation is satisfied
- Cash received before performance = unearned revenue (liability)
- Performance before cash = accounts receivable (asset)
