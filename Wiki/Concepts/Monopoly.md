# Monopoly

## Definition

A monopoly is a market with a single seller facing the entire market demand curve. The monopolist has market power — the ability to set price above marginal cost.

## Key Features

- **MR < P**: the monopolist's marginal revenue curve lies below the demand curve
- **Profit maximization**: produce where MR = MC, charge the price consumers are willing to pay
- **Deadweight loss**: monopoly output < competitive output → welfare loss
- **Barriers to entry**: legal (patents, licenses), natural (economies of scale), strategic

## Price Discrimination

- **1st degree**: charge each consumer their willingness to pay (captures all surplus)
- **2nd degree**: different prices for different quantities (volume discounts)
- **3rd degree**: different prices for different groups (student discounts)
