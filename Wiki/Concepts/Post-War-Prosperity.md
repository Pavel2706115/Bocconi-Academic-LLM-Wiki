# Post-War Prosperity

## Definition

The period from 1945 to 1973 — often called the "Golden Age of Capitalism" or "Les Trente Glorieuses" — was characterised by unprecedented economic growth, full employment, and rising living standards in Western economies.

## Drivers

- Post-war reconstruction (Marshall Plan)
- Stable international monetary system (Bretton Woods)
- Technological catch-up and diffusion
- Expansion of welfare states
- Baby boom and labor supply growth
- Keynesian demand management

## End of the Golden Age

- Oil shocks (1973, 1979)
- Stagflation (simultaneous inflation and unemployment)
- Breakdown of Bretton Woods
- Productivity slowdown
