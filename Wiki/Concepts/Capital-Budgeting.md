# Capital Budgeting

## Definition

Capital budgeting is the process of evaluating and selecting long-term investment projects. It involves estimating incremental cash flows, adjusting for inflation and taxes, and applying appropriate discount rates.

## Practical Considerations

### Inflation
- Discount nominal cash flows with nominal rates, or real cash flows with real rates
- 1 + r_real = (1 + r_nominal) / (1 + inflation)

### Depreciation Tax Shield
- Depreciation reduces taxable income
- Tax Shield = Depreciation × Corporate Tax Rate

### Working Capital
- Changes in Net Working Capital (NWC) are cash flows
- Increases in inventory/receivables drain cash; increases in payables retain cash

### Incremental Cash Flows
- Only include cash flows that change as a result of the project
- Exclude sunk costs; include opportunity costs
