# Break-Even Analysis

## Definition

Break-even analysis determines the level of sales at which total revenue equals total cost, resulting in zero profit. Beyond this point, each additional unit sold generates profit.

## Formula

$$\text{Break-Even Quantity} = \frac{\text{Fixed Costs}}{\text{Price per Unit} − \text{Variable Cost per Unit}}$$

*   **Contribution margin**: $\text{Price} − \text{Variable Cost per unit}$
*   **Contribution margin ratio**: $\frac{\text{Contribution margin}}{\text{Price}}$

## Applications

*   Setting sales targets
*   Evaluating pricing strategies
*   Assessing the impact of cost changes on profitability
*   Make-or-buy decisions

---

## Marketing Application

In marketing, break-even analysis is the fundamental tool for **cost-based pricing** and evaluating promotional campaigns.

### 1. Target Break-Even Price
Firms determine the minimum price required to break even at a projected sales volume ($Q$):
$$\text{BEP}_{\text{Price}} = \text{Variable Cost per Unit} + \frac{\text{Fixed Costs}}{Q}$$

### 2. Multi-Unit Product Bundling
Firms often sell products in packages or bundles containing multiple units (e.g. multi-packs). Contribution margin is calculated on two distinct levels:
*   **Unit Contribution Margin**: $\text{Price per unit} - \text{Variable Cost per unit}$
*   **Package Contribution Margin**: $\text{Price per package} - \text{Variable Cost per package}$
    *   Where $\text{Variable Cost per package} = (\text{Variable Cost per unit} \times \text{Units per package}) + \text{Packaging materials cost}$.
    *   Marketers must evaluate the break-even point in terms of *packages* when designing promotional multi-packs.
