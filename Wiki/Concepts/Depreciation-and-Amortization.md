# Depreciation and Amortization

## Definition

Depreciation systematically allocates the cost of tangible long-lived assets (buildings, equipment) over their useful life. Amortization does the same for intangible assets (patents, goodwill).

## Methods

- **Straight-line**: equal expense each year = (Cost − Residual) / Useful life
- **Declining balance**: accelerated — higher expense in early years
- **Units-of-production**: based on actual usage

## Key Points

- Depreciation is a non-cash expense — it reduces reported income but does not consume cash
- In the cash flow statement (indirect method), depreciation is **added back** to net income
- Creates a **tax shield**: Tax savings = Depreciation × Tax rate
