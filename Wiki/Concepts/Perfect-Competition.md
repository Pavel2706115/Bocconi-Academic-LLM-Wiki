# Perfect Competition

## Definition

Perfect competition is a market structure where many small firms sell identical products, no firm can influence the market price, and there are no barriers to entry or exit.

## Characteristics

- Many buyers and sellers
- Homogeneous product
- Perfect information
- Free entry and exit
- Price-taking behaviour

## Short-Run Equilibrium

- Firm produces where P = MC (as long as P ≥ AVC)
- Profits: π = (P − ATC) × Q
- Shutdown condition: P < AVC

## Long-Run Equilibrium

- Economic profits attract entry → supply shifts right → price falls
- Economic losses cause exit → supply shifts left → price rises
- Long-run: P = MC = min(ATC) → zero economic profit
