# Financial System Overview

## Definition

The financial system channels funds from surplus units (savers) to deficit units (borrowers) through two channels: direct finance (financial markets) and indirect finance (financial intermediaries).

## Two Segments

### Direct Finance (Financial Markets)
Borrowers sell securities directly to lenders in financial markets (e.g., a corporation issues stock to investors).

### Indirect Finance (Financial Intermediaries)
An intermediary (e.g., a bank) collects funds from savers and lends them to borrowers.

## Market Classifications

| Dimension | Categories |
|-----------|------------|
| By transacting parties | Primary market (new issues) vs Secondary market (existing securities) |
| By security type | Money markets (<1 yr) vs Capital markets (≥1 yr) |
| By instrument | Debt vs Equity vs Derivatives vs FX |

## Why Financial Intermediaries Exist

1. **Transaction costs**: economies of scale in financial transactions
2. **Risk sharing**: diversification and asset transformation
3. **Asymmetric information**: specialized screening and monitoring to mitigate adverse selection and moral hazard
4. **Economies of scope**: same information used for multiple financial services
