# Social Insurance

## Definition

Social insurance programs are government-provided insurance against adverse events (unemployment, disability, old age, illness). They exist because private insurance markets often fail due to adverse selection and moral hazard.

## Why Government Provision?

- **Adverse selection**: healthy/low-risk individuals opt out → private premiums spiral
- **Moral hazard**: insurance reduces incentive for care → excessive risk-taking
- **Mandatory participation**: solves adverse selection by pooling all risks together

## Key Programs

- Unemployment insurance
- Disability insurance
- Social Security / pensions
- Health insurance

## Trade-offs

- **Coverage vs incentives**: generous benefits reduce work incentives
- **Equity vs efficiency**: redistribution creates deadweight loss
