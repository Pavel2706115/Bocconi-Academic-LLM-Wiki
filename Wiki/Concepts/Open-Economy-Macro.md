# Open Economy Macroeconomics

## Definition

Open economy macroeconomics extends the IS-LM framework to include international trade and capital flows. Exchange rates, trade balances, and capital mobility add new dimensions to policy analysis.

## Key Variables

- **Nominal exchange rate (E)**: price of foreign currency in domestic currency
- **Real exchange rate (ε)**: E × P*/P — competitiveness measure
- **Net exports (NX)**: exports − imports; depends on real exchange rate and domestic/foreign income
- **Capital account**: net financial inflows; depends on interest rate differentials

## Open Economy IS-LM

- IS curve includes NX: higher domestic income → more imports → smaller NX
- Interest rate parity: domestic i must equal foreign i* plus expected depreciation
- **Mundell-Fleming model**: fiscal/monetary policy effectiveness depends on exchange rate regime
