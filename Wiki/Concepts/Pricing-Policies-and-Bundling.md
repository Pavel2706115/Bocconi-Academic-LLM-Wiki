# Pricing Policies and Bundling

## Definition

**Pricing Strategy** is the process of setting and managing the prices of a company's offerings to capture value, achieve sales volume targets, and establish competitive positioning. Pricing is the only element of the marketing mix that generates revenue (all other elements represent costs). Effective pricing must coordinate three bounds: **cost boundaries** (the floor), **competitors' prices** (the baseline), and **consumer value perception** (the ceiling).

---

## 1. Price Setting Criteria

Firms establish prices using three primary perspectives:

### A. Cost-Based Pricing (Cost-Volume-Profit)
Firms set prices based on cost calculations to achieve targeted margins or break-even volume.
*   **Target-Return / Markup Pricing**:
    $$\text{Price} = \frac{\text{Unit Cost}}{1 - \text{Desired Margin}}$$
    *   Where $\text{Unit Cost} = \text{Variable Cost per Unit} + \frac{\text{Fixed Costs}}{\text{Sales Volume}}$.
*   **Break-even Price**: The price at which total revenue equals total costs for a given sales volume ($Q$):
    $$\text{BEP}_{\text{Price}} = \text{Variable Cost per Unit} + \frac{\text{Fixed Costs}}{Q}$$

### B. Competition-Based Pricing (Indifference Price)
Sets prices relative to competitors, adjusting for perceived quality/attribute differences.
*   **Indifference Price**: The maximum price a consumer is willing to pay for a company's product such that they are indifferent between buying it and buying a competitor's product.
*   It is calculated using multi-attribute expected value ratings (e.g. Fishbein expected values):
    $$\text{Price}_{\text{Indifference}} = \text{Price}_{\text{Competitor}} + \text{Value of Perceived Advantage}$$
    $$\text{Price}_{\text{Indifference}} = \text{Price}_{\text{Competitor}} \times \left( \frac{\text{Attitude Index}_{\text{Company}}}{\text{Attitude Index}_{\text{Competitor}}} \right)$$

### C. Demand/Value-Based Pricing
Sets prices based on the customer's perceived value of the product's benefits, independent of cost.

---

## 2. Price Differentiation & Discrimination

Firms manage their price policies to extract maximum consumer surplus from different market segments.

### A. Bundling vs. Unbundling
*   **Bundling (Product Tie-in)**: Selling two or more distinct products together as a single package for a combined price that is typically lower than the sum of their individual prices (e.g. software suites, fast-food meals). Exploits consumer heterogeneous valuations to maximize total contribution margin.
*   **Unbundling**: Breaking down a product range into individual, separately priced components (e.g. low-cost airlines charging separately for seat selection, luggage, and meals).

### B. Price Discrimination
Charging different prices to different consumers for the exact same product or service based on:
1.  *Customer Profile* (e.g., student or senior discounts).
2.  *Purchase Location* (e.g., higher prices in airports vs. local supermarkets).
3.  *Timing of Purchase* (e.g., dynamic pricing for flights, happy hours).
4.  *Quantity Purchased* (e.g., volume discounts, block tariffs).

### C. Skimming vs. Penetration Pricing
When launching new products, firms choose between two competing pricing paths:
*   **Skimming Pricing**: Setting a high initial price to target price-insensitive, high-value segments ("skimming the cream"), gradually lowering the price over time to capture broader segments. Used for highly unique or technologically advanced products.
*   **Penetration Pricing**: Setting a very low initial price to capture high market share rapidly, create high barriers to entry, and build economies of scale. Used in price-sensitive markets.

---

## 3. Channel Relationship Pricing

When selling through commercial intermediaries, manufacturers manage prices to motivate distributors and secure point-of-sale support:
*   **Discounts & Bonuses**:
    *   *Trade Margin / Sell-in Discount*: The margin granted to distributors (the difference between distributor sell-out price and manufacturer sell-in price).
    *   *Quantity Discount*: Reductions in sell-in price for large order volumes.
    *   *End-of-Year Bonus*: Performance-based payouts for reaching annual sales targets.
