# Great Divergence

## Definition

The Great Divergence refers to the period (roughly 1500-1800) when Western Europe pulled ahead of the rest of the world in economic development, ultimately leading to the Industrial Revolution.

## Explanatory Factors

- **Institutions**: property rights, rule of law, parliamentary governance
- **Geography**: access to coal, navigable rivers, temperate climate
- **Culture**: scientific revolution, Enlightenment values, commercial culture
- **Competition between states**: Europe's fragmented political landscape spurred innovation
- **Colonial exploitation**: resource extraction and trade surpluses

## Debate

- Kenneth Pomeranz: Europe and China were comparable until ~1750; coal and colonies explain the divergence
- Douglass North: institutional foundations (secure property rights) were the key driver
