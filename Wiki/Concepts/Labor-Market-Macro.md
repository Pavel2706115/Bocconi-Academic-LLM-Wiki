# Labor Market

## Definition

The labor market determines wages and employment. In the Blanchard framework, the equilibrium unemployment rate (natural rate) is determined by the intersection of wage-setting and price-setting relations.

## Wage-Setting (WS)

W = Pᵉ × F(u, z), where:
- u = unemployment rate (higher u → lower bargaining power → lower wages)
- z = labor market institutions (unemployment benefits, employment protection)

## Price-Setting (PS)

P = (1+μ) × W / A, where μ is the markup over marginal cost.
- Real wage implied: W/P = A / (1+μ)

## Natural Rate of Unemployment

Where WS = PS: the unemployment rate consistent with stable inflation.
