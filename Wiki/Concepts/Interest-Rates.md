# Interest Rates

## Definition

Interest rates are the price of borrowing money (or the return on lending). They are the most fundamental variable in finance, influencing investment decisions, asset prices, consumption, and monetary policy.

## Measuring Interest Rates

- **Yield to Maturity (YTM)**: the interest rate that equates the PV of a bond's cash flows to its current price
- **Current Yield**: annual coupon / current price
- **Real vs Nominal**: real rate = nominal rate − inflation (Fisher equation)

## Term Structure of Interest Rates

- **Yield curve**: plots yields against maturity
- **Normal (upward sloping)**: long-term rates > short-term rates
- **Inverted**: short-term rates > long-term rates — often predicts recession
- **Expectations hypothesis**: long rates reflect expected future short rates
- **Liquidity premium theory**: investors demand extra compensation for holding longer maturities

## Risk Structure

- **Default risk premium**: compensation for credit risk
- **Liquidity premium**: compensation for illiquidity
- **Tax treatment**: municipal bonds offer lower yields due to tax exemption
