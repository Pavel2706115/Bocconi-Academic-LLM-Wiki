# Expectations in Macroeconomics

## Definition

Expectations about future economic variables (inflation, interest rates, income) fundamentally affect current decisions. How agents form expectations determines the effectiveness of policy.

## Where Expectations Matter

- **Bond markets**: long-term interest rates reflect expected future short rates
- **Stock markets**: stock prices equal expected discounted future dividends
- **Consumption**: forward-looking consumers base spending on permanent (lifetime) income, not just current income
- **Investment**: firms invest based on expected future profitability (Tobin's q)

## Expectations Formation

- **Adaptive**: agents extrapolate from past values
- **Rational**: agents use all available information optimally

## Policy Implications

When expectations are rational, anticipated policy changes may have no real effects (policy ineffectiveness proposition), while unanticipated changes do.
