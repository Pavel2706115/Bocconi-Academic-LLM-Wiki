# Pre-Industrial Economy

## Definition

The pre-industrial economy (before c. 1750) was characterised by agricultural dominance, low productivity growth, and the Malthusian trap — where population growth consumed any increase in output, keeping living standards stagnant.

## Malthusian Model

- Population ↑ → land per capita ↓ → diminishing returns → living standards ↓
- Positive checks (famine, disease) and preventive checks (delayed marriage) limit population
- The Black Death (1347-1351) paradoxically raised living standards by reducing population relative to land

## Key Features

- Agriculture employed 80-90% of the population
- Limited trade and specialization
- Slow technological change
- Rigid social hierarchies and guilds
