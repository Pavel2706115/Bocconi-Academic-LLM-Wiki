# Banking System

## Definition

Banks are financial intermediaries that accept deposits from savers and make loans to borrowers. They transform assets (short-term deposits into long-term loans) and provide liquidity services to the economy.

## Bank Balance Sheet

| Assets | Liabilities |
|--------|-------------|
| Reserves | Deposits |
| Loans | Borrowings |
| Securities | Bank capital (equity) |

## Key Risks

- **Credit risk**: borrower default
- **Interest rate risk**: mismatch between asset and liability durations
- **Liquidity risk**: inability to meet deposit withdrawal demands
- **Off-balance-sheet risk**: guarantees, derivatives, securitization

## Types of Institutions

- **Commercial banks**: deposits + loans (core banking)
- **Savings institutions (thrifts)**: specialized in mortgage lending
- **Credit unions**: member-owned, non-profit
- **Investment banks**: securities underwriting, advisory, trading
